## 2) Topic facts with citation anchors

### Confirmed facts (directly stated in sources)
1. **Permit issuance is conditioned on CCB licensing status when licensure is required.** A city/county/state agency may not issue a building permit to a person required to be licensed unless that person has a current valid properly endorsed CCB license; permit applicants must sign a statement either giving license number (if contractor) or basis for exemption. **[S1]**
2. **For ORS 701.010(6)-exempt applicants, agencies must provide the owner-responsibility notice and obtain signed acknowledgment before issuing a residential building permit.** **[S1]**
3. **The adopted owner statement includes commitments about residency/occupancy and using licensed contractors/subcontractors if hired.** **[S1][S3]**
4. **The CCB-adopted owner statement form is also used in practice for residential building, electrical, mechanical, and plumbing permits.** (Form language). **[S3]**
5. **OAR 812-001-0200 formally adopts the “Information Notice to Property Owners About Construction Responsibilities” form under ORS 701.325(3).** **[S2]**
6. **PSI bulletin exam blueprint includes “Oregon Const. Contractor Laws and Regulations” and “Oregon Building Codes” content areas, making permit/compliance literacy test-relevant.** **[S4]**
7. **PSI bulletin specifies exam format: 80 scored items, 180 minutes, passing at 70%, plus 8 non-scored items.** **[S4]**

### Inference (reasoned, but not explicitly guaranteed in one sentence by source)
1. **A permit-focused lesson should emphasize both legal gatekeeping (license/exemption statement) and owner obligations after permit issuance** because statute + form combine these points. (Inference from ORS 701.325 + form content). **[S1][S3]**
2. **Exam prep likely benefits from scenario questions at permit counter** (licensed contractor applicant vs owner-builder exempt applicant), because exam outline includes laws/regulations/building codes rather than only pure definitions. **[S4]**
3. **Course language should avoid overgeneralizing from “building permit” to all permit types unless tied to adopted form language or another statute/rule** because ORS text is framed around building permits while the form references broader residential permit categories. **[S1][S3]**

## 3) Exact claims safe to teach

These are tightly bounded statements that can be taught with low legal overreach risk:

1. “If Oregon law requires you to hold a CCB license for the work, the permit office cannot issue your building permit unless you have a current, valid, properly endorsed license.” **[S1]**
2. “Permit applicants must sign a statement at permit issuance: contractors provide license number; exempt applicants state their exemption basis.” **[S1]**
3. “If an applicant is exempt under ORS 701.010(6), the agency must provide the owner construction responsibilities notice and collect a signed acknowledgment before issuing a residential building permit.” **[S1]**
4. “The owner statement form says that if the owner hires contractors/subcontractors, those hired parties must be licensed with the CCB and properly endorsed.” **[S1][S3]**
5. “The CCB/PSI exam includes Oregon contractor laws/regulations and Oregon building-code content; it is not only business-administration material.” **[S4]**

Claims that should **always** carry citations when published on course pages:
- Any “must/may not” legal requirement wording.
- Any statement about who can receive permits under owner exemption pathways.
- Any exam-structure numbers (item count, time, passing score, fee).
- Any statement that the owner statement applies to electrical/mechanical/plumbing permits (because this is form language and may change by revision). **[S3]**

## 4) Unresolved ambiguities / gaps in public sources

1. **Building permit vs other permit types:** ORS 701.325 text is centered on building permits, while the CCB form states it is required for residential building/electrical/mechanical/plumbing permits. Public-facing sources do not, in one place, fully reconcile the scope mechanics by permit type and agency workflow. **[S1][S3]**
2. **“Substantial improvement” threshold in homeowner notice context:** the notice references substantial improvements, but public text here does not define a clear threshold in the same document. **[S3]**
3. **Agency implementation variance:** statute sets conditions, but local process details (e.g., exact form handling in ePermitting portals) are not standardized in the cited documents.
4. **Exam blueprint depth:** PSI gives topic buckets and counts but not detailed sub-objectives for permitting scenarios; course writers must avoid claiming fine-grained tested subtopics unless separately sourced. **[S4]**

## 6) Recommended boundaries for course assertions

### Course **should** assert
- Statutory permit-gatekeeping rules exactly as written (license required ⇒ no permit without valid endorsed license). **[S1]**
- Signed applicant statement requirement and ORS 701.010(6) owner acknowledgement workflow. **[S1]**
- Owner responsibilities language tied directly to adopted CCB form (inspection notifications, using licensed contractors if hiring, etc.). **[S3]**
- PSI exam’s high-level tested areas and published test structure numbers. **[S4]**

### Course **should not** assert (without additional primary authority)
- A universal one-size-fits-all rule that every residential permit type always uses identical owner-exemption processing in every jurisdiction.
- Detailed legal definitions (e.g., “substantial improvement” thresholds) that are not present in these cited sources.
- Hyper-specific claims about how many exam questions will appear on a particular permitting subtopic (PSI provides category counts, not micro-topic guarantees). **[S4]**
- Legal advice framing such as “you are definitely exempt” for individual fact patterns; instead present decision pathways and referral to CCB/local building department.
