# Tradeschool Curriculum: Building a House in Ashland, Oregon

From land acquisition to lifelong ownership, with a capstone into Oregon Residential General Contractor license preparation.

## Program purpose

Train students from zero to competent residential builder-developer-operator for single-family home delivery in Ashland, Oregon, including land acquisition, planning, design, permitting, site development, construction, turnover, maintenance, and business readiness for Oregon CCB residential general contractor licensure.

## I. Profession map: major professions and mastery domains

### 1) Land acquisition and real estate development
- Roles: land acquisition analyst, real estate broker, developer, owner’s representative, due diligence manager.
- Mastery domains: site selection, highest and best use, title/easements/encumbrances, purchase contingencies and escrow, access/frontage, utility and water/sewer/septic feasibility, zoning/overlay screening, floodplain/steep slope/wildfire/soils/tree/historic risk screening, pro forma and residual land value, timeline forecasting, hearing risk, insurance and risk allocation.

### 2) Land use and entitlement planning
- Roles: land use planner, planning consultant, entitlement manager, urban designer, hearing preparation specialist.
- Mastery domains: Ashland planning process, pre-application conference packets, site design review, Type I and related actions, conditional use, variance/exception logic, overlay standards, wildfire lands standards, hillside and environmental constraints, historic triggers, tree preservation/removal, findings writing, outreach/testimony strategy, staff report interpretation, conditions tracking.
- Ashland competency anchor: pre-application conferences are the first step and include multi-department circulation (electric, fire, building, water, sewer, engineering).

### 3) Surveying and geospatial services
- Roles: professional land surveyor, boundary survey technician, construction staking technician, GIS technician.
- Mastery domains: boundary/topographic surveys, ALTA/title coordination, easement plotting, legal descriptions, staking, grading references, utility locate coordination, GIS parcel interpretation, slope mapping, benchmark transfer, as-builts.

### 4) Environmental and natural resource consulting
- Roles: environmental consultant, wetland consultant, arborist, forestry/wildfire consultant, erosion control specialist, habitat consultant.
- Mastery domains: tree inventory and protection, removal documentation, defensible space, prohibited plants, erosion/sediment control, stormwater basics, site restoration, environmental constraints mapping, wildfire mitigation in design/site work.
- Ashland competency anchor: citywide wildfire ordinance, wildfire lands standards, and tree protection rules shape development and site work.

### 5) Geotechnical and soils engineering
- Roles: geotechnical engineer, engineering geologist, soils technician.
- Mastery domains: classification, bearing capacity, expansive soils, settlement, slope stability, foundation and retaining recommendations, groundwater observations, compaction testing, fill acceptance, excavation recommendations, seismic considerations.

### 6) Civil engineering
- Roles: civil engineer, site development engineer, grading/drainage designer, utility engineer.
- Mastery domains: grading/drainage, driveway geometry, culvert/ditch logic, utility routing, stormwater conveyance, erosion control plans, right-of-way improvements, street cuts, access/fire coordination, earthwork quantities, feasibility optimization.

### 7) Architecture and residential design
- Roles: architect, residential designer, building designer, draftsperson, BIM/CAD technician.
- Mastery domains: programming through CDs, life safety and ORSC research, space planning, envelope detailing, openings schedules, roof geometry, energy/water/fire resilience detailing, permit set standards, finish specs, interdisciplinary coordination.

### 8) Structural engineering
- Roles: structural engineer, framing designer, lateral systems specialist.
- Mastery domains: gravity/lateral systems, seismic/wind basics, foundation/floor/roof systems, shear and hold-downs, openings/headers, connections, retaining, deck/stair design, unusual geometry.

### 9) MEP engineering/design
- Roles: mechanical designer, HVAC contractor/designer, plumbing designer, electrical designer, lighting designer, renewable systems designer.
- Mastery domains: load concepts, ventilation, duct design, equipment selection, domestic water/DWV, service sizing, panel schedules, controls, IAQ, water heating, heat pumps, PV and battery readiness, framing/envelope coordination.

### 10) Energy and building science
- Roles: energy consultant, enclosure consultant, HERS specialist, commissioning-minded builder.
- Mastery domains: thermal boundary, air/vapor/moisture control, windows/insulation, bridge reduction, IAQ and ventilation, Oregon energy pathway awareness, durability and condensation risk, wildfire-resistant enclosure details.

### 11) Permitting and code administration
- Roles: permit expediter, code consultant, plan review coordinator, inspection coordinator.
- Mastery domains: package assembly, sequencing, revisions, inspections, deferred submittals, fee forecasting, correction responses, alternate methods, closeout/final approval path.
- Ashland competency anchor: full plan review/inspection services, online portal workflows, 24-hour inspection notice, and online submission across building/planning/public works permit types.

### 12) Specialty permitting and agency coordination
- Roles: utility coordinator, septic permit specialist, right-of-way coordinator, fire review coordinator.
- Mastery domains: utility applications, water/sewer coordination, right-of-way closures, street excavation, miscellaneous concrete permits, septic site evaluation/install permits, fire access coordination, local special applications, integrated review schedules.
- Oregon/Ashland anchor: Jackson County septic workflow routes through DEQ Medford (site evaluation plus separate installation permit; one-year validity), and Ashland requires a Carbon Pollution Impact application for new residential units.

### 13) Estimating, preconstruction, and procurement
- Roles: estimator, preconstruction manager, purchasing agent, procurement coordinator.
- Mastery domains: takeoff, unit/assembly pricing, labor assumptions, bid leveling, scope-gap analysis, VE, long-lead procurement, buyout, cash flow, contingencies, change pricing, supplier management.

### 14) Project management
- Roles: residential PM, assistant PM, scheduler, document controller.
- Mastery domains: CPM/pull planning basics, sequencing, permit critical path, risk register, budget control, RFI/submittal/change workflows, meetings, owner communication, daily logs, quality hold points, turnover planning.

### 15) Site supervision
- Roles: superintendent, assistant superintendent, foreman.
- Mastery domains: field coordination, trade stacking, logistics, sequencing, safety, layout verification, inspection readiness, receiving, punch control, weather/neighbor planning, productivity, quality enforcement, subcontractor accountability.

### 16) Safety and regulatory compliance
- Roles: safety manager, competent person, OSHA trainer, risk manager.
- Mastery domains: hazard identification, fall and excavation safety, ladder/scaffold and electrical safety, PPE, housekeeping, incident reporting, emergency response, smoke/heat planning, documentation.

### 17) Trade professions (core craft mastery)
- Demolition and clearing
- Earthwork and excavation
- Concrete and foundations
- Framing
- Roofing
- Exterior envelope
- Insulation and air sealing
- Plumbing
- Electrical
- Mechanical/HVAC
- Drywall and interior finishes
- Exterior site improvements

### 18) Historic preservation and contextual design
- Roles: historic consultant, preservation architect, review coordinator.
- Mastery domains: district applicability, compatibility standards, SOI awareness, repair vs replacement logic, exterior change review triggers, contextual infill.
- Ashland anchor: in historic contexts, exterior changes requiring building permits may also require separate land use approval.

### 19) Eccentric / experimental / high-design delivery
- Roles: design-build lead, specialty fabricator, parametric designer, alternate methods coordinator.
- Mastery domains: geometry rationalization, custom budget discipline, prototypes/mockups, alternate methods, specialty engineering, means/methods planning, unconventional materials, fabrication integration, long-lead management, expectation control.

### 20) Finance, accounting, and lending
- Roles: lender, draw administrator, cost accountant, controller, bookkeeper.
- Mastery domains: budgets, draws, SOV, lien waivers, job costing, cash flow, payroll awareness, retainage, vendor terms, audit trail, tax basis, claims support.

### 21) Legal and contract administration
- Roles: construction attorney, contract administrator, claims manager.
- Mastery domains: prime/sub contracts, scope exhibits, insurance/bonds, change order language, delays, indemnity basics, defect claims, warranties, lien awareness, dispute records.

### 22) Sales, client management, and handover
- Roles: client advisor, selections coordinator, handover manager, warranty administrator.
- Mastery domains: discovery/programming, budget communication, selections/allowances, change management, expectation alignment, owner training, punch walkthroughs, warranty manuals, service response.

### 23) Long-term homeownership and stewardship
- Roles: homeowner, estate manager, facilities manager, maintenance coordinator, retrofit planner.
- Mastery domains: preventive maintenance cycles, roof/drainage upkeep, envelope checks, HVAC/plumbing/electrical maintenance awareness, wildfire hardening upkeep, vegetation management, retrofit economics, reserves, aging-in-place adaptation.

### 24) Oregon contractor licensing and business operations
- Roles: Responsible Managing Individual, residential GC owner, compliance manager.
- Mastery domains: CCB path, endorsement selection, RMI duties, pre-license education and exam prep, bond/liability requirements, workers’ comp awareness, entity setup, records, contract compliance, complaint/claims awareness, renewals.
- Oregon anchor: CCB licensure requires an RMI, 16 hours of approved pre-license training, Oregon exam passage, and an application package including bond/insurance/signatures/fee. Residential GC benchmark values include a $25,000 bond and $500,000 per-occurrence liability insurance.

## II. Program structure

### Tiers
1. Foundation Certificate
2. Site and Shell Diploma
3. Full House Delivery Diploma
4. Capstone: Oregon Residential General Contractor Prep

### Delivery model
- Classroom, lab/shop, simulations, field visits, permit set studios
- Live project practicum, apprenticeship rotations, code/contract seminars
- Maintenance residency after turnover

### Graduate outcomes
Graduates can evaluate parcels, map approvals, coordinate design/engineering, estimate and schedule, supervise field delivery, manage inspections/closeout, hand over durable code-compliant homes, and prepare for Oregon residential GC licensure.

1. Ashland Parcel Feasibility
2. Pre-Application Conference Package
3. Full Permit Set for a Single-Family Home
4. Eccentric House Delivery (comparative)
5. Turnover and Maintenance Manual

## VI. Apprenticeship rotations
Fifteen rotations spanning permit observation, survey/layout, excavation/utilities, foundation, framing, roof/envelope, plumbing/electrical/HVAC rough-in, insulation/drywall, interiors, landscaping/defensible space, PM/estimating, warranty/service calls, and homeowner maintenance walkthrough.

## VII. Competency standards by graduate level
- Foundation: lifecycle fluency, basic plan reading, parcel fatal-flaw screening, permitting awareness, basic takeoff/scheduling.
- Site and Shell: due diligence, pre-app coordination, permit-ready info, estimate/schedule, supervision through dry-in.
- Full House Delivery: end-to-end phase management, inspections/corrections, budget/schedule/quality/safety control, turnover and warranty operations.
- Capstone: assistant PM/superintendent/design-build coordinator readiness, contractor business organization, Oregon licensing prep, full-project supervision.

## VIII. Oregon Residential General Contractor license-prep capstone
- Capstone 1: CCB Licensing Framework
- Capstone 2: Pre-License Education Integration
- Capstone 3: Insurance, Bonding, and Business Formation
- Capstone 4: Oregon Exam Drill Lab
- Capstone 5: Final Integrated House Delivery Simulation

Benchmark prep facts to reinforce in coursework:
- Approved pre-license training: minimum 16 hours.
- Oregon exam format: open-book, 80 multiple-choice items, 70% passing score, 3-hour limit.
- Residential GC benchmarks: $25,000 bond, $500,000 per-occurrence liability insurance, and current CCB application fee requirements.

## IX. Graduation requirements
Students must produce:
1. Ashland parcel feasibility study
2. Pre-application conference package
3. Full permit set for a code-compliant single-family home
4. Eccentric-design alternative with risk/cost comparison
5. Full estimate and schedule
6. Field-quality and inspection binder
7. Turnover and 10-year maintenance manual
8. Oregon CCB license-prep portfolio

## X. Final curriculum outcome

Graduates can execute the entire chain:

**buy land → screen constraints → complete pre-app/entitlement → coordinate survey/soils/design → assemble Ashland permits → manage utilities/septic or sewer/ROW/grading → build through foundations, shell, MEP, finishes, and site work → pass inspections → hand over the home → manage long-term maintenance and upgrades → transition into Oregon residential GC license preparation.**
