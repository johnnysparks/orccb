# Curriculum Map

Topic list derived from the public PSI Candidate Information Bulletin for the Oregon Construction Contractors exam.

## Exam format

- **Questions**: 80 multiple choice
- **Time**: 3 hours
- **Format**: Open book
- **Passing score**: 70% (56 of 80)

## Ordering rationale

1. **Laws and Regulations** first — it frames the entire licensing context and has the highest exam weight (~18%).
2. **Business Structure and Employees** next — foundational business concepts that inform contract and subcontractor topics.
3. **Contracts** — high-weight topic that depends on understanding the regulatory context.
4. **Lien Law** — Oregon-specific and frequently tested; placed after contracts for natural flow.
5. **Bidding, Scheduling, Codes, Safety** — operational topics grouped by project lifecycle.
6. **Financial and Tax** last — standalone topics that don't gate other material.

## First 3 topics for launch

| Topic | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Laws and Regulations | Highest exam weight (~18%). Sets the regulatory foundation for everything else. |
| Contracts | Second-highest weight (~12%). High practical value and lots of testable detail. |
| Hiring and Managing Employees | Third-highest weight (~10%). Covers employer obligations, a common exam trap area. |

Together these 3 topics account for roughly **40% of the exam** and give the site enough substance to be credible at launch.

## Weight disclaimer

Exam weight percentages are **estimates** derived from the PSI content outline structure. They are not official percentages published by PSI or the Oregon CCB. These estimates will be refined as topic research progresses and primary sources are reviewed.
