## 2) Topic facts with citation anchors (confirmed facts vs inference)

### Confirmed facts (directly supported)

1. The PSI Oregon CCB exam outline includes **"Bidding and Estimating" with 6 scored items**. **[T1-1]**
2. Under ORS 701.305, a contractor may not perform covered residential work for an owner without a written contract when aggregate contract price exceeds **$2,000**; if work starts below $2,000 but later exceeds it, written contract delivery is required within **five days** after the contractor knows or should know the threshold is exceeded. **[T1-2]**
3. ORS 701.310 provides a residential owner cancellation right until midnight of the next business day after entering an initial written contract, with statutory exceptions. **[T1-2]**
4. CCB's Notices Chart reiterates written-contract timing over the $2,000 threshold and references ORS 701.330 / OAR consumer-notice rules tied to contract execution. **[T1-3]**
5. CCB consumer guidance distinguishes **fixed-price bid** versus **time-and-materials** pricing and warns that time-and-materials may not provide a known final total price at contract formation. **[T2-1]**
6. CCB continuing-education topic categories explicitly include **construction business practices, including bidding and estimating**. **[T2-2]**

### Inference (synthesis, not verbatim)

1. The exam likely tests bidding/estimating as a **business-practices competency** (not only math), because the official topic appears in the PSI outline and CCB education taxonomy under business practices. **Inference from [T1-1], [T2-2].**
2. Oregon source material supports teaching **estimate-to-contract compliance sequencing** (pricing method choice -> written contract threshold -> notice/cancellation requirements), rather than generic national estimating formulas alone. **Inference from [T1-2], [T1-3], [T2-1].**
3. For exam prep, legal risk in bidding/estimating appears concentrated at the **conversion point from estimate/bid to signed residential contract**, where ORS timing and notice obligations activate. **Inference from [T1-2], [T1-3].**

## 6) Recommended boundaries for what the course should and should not assert

### Should assert

1. The **exam presence and size** of M4 (6 items), without claiming unpublished PSI subtopic weighting. **[T1-1]**
2. Oregon **estimate-to-contract compliance triggers** that are explicit in statute (written contract threshold, five-day rule if threshold is crossed, cancellation window and exceptions). **[T1-2]**
3. Practical distinction between **fixed-price** and **time-and-materials** as a consumer/business risk concept. **[T2-1]**
4. That Oregon CCB frames bidding/estimating as a recognized **business-practices** competency area. **[T2-2]**

### Should NOT assert (without stronger primary support)

1. Exact numeric rules-of-thumb (e.g., "always use X% overhead" or "standard Oregon markup is Y%") as if legally mandated.
2. Any claim that PSI publishes a detailed M4 subdomain weighting or exact competency breakdown beyond the 6-item domain count. **[T1-1]**
3. Guaranteed legal outcomes for specific bid/estimate disputes (e.g., always enforceable, always recoverable damages) without fact-specific legal authority.
4. Oregon-specific requirements for advanced bidding instruments (public procurement bid bonds, alternates, GMP mechanics) unless anchored to the exact controlling statute/rule for the scenario being taught.
