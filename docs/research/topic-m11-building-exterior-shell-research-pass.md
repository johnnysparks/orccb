## 2) Topic facts with citation anchors

### Confirmed facts (high confidence)

1. Oregon’s contractor exam is administered through PSI for the CCB licensing process. **([S1], [S2])**
2. The CCB exam is open-book and tied to the **Oregon version of the NASCLA Contractors Guide to Business, Law and Project Management (2nd ed.)**. **([S1], [S2])**
3. Exam structure currently published by CCB: **80 multiple-choice questions**, **70% passing score (56 correct)**, **3 hours**, **$60**. **([S1])**
4. PSI bulletin states the exam questions are based on that NASCLA Oregon manual and that the test covers **laws and business practices**. **([S2])**
5. Oregon’s residential code adoption page indicates an active cycle with **2026 ORSC adoption process** and an anticipated adoption date of **October 1, 2026**, based on the 2024 IRC with Oregon amendments. **([S3])**
6. Oregon code text (as surfaced in the ORSC significant-changes PDF) includes exterior-envelope requirements such as weather-resistant exterior wall envelope, drainage plane concepts, and integrated flashing under **R703.1/R703.1.1/R703.4** references. **([S4])**
7. ORS chapter 455 is the state building code chapter and includes statewide uniformity/preemption framework for code requirements. **([S5], [S7])**
8. ORS chapter 701 governs Oregon construction contractor licensing/legal framework. **([S6], [S2])**

### Inference (explicitly not directly confirmed as exam-blueprint detail)

1. “M11 Building Exterior Shell” likely sits in a technical/applied-construction competency area for your curriculum and may map to envelope principles (water management, flashing, drainage, weather resistance). This is inferred from ORSC envelope sections and common construction education structure, **not** from a public PSI domain-level content outline that names “M11.” **(Inference from [S2], [S4])**
2. If the Oregon exam is still primarily “laws and business practices” per PSI, then deep prescriptive envelope code details may be better taught as contractor-practice enrichment than as core test-prep unless internal blueprint evidence says otherwise. **(Inference from [S2])**
