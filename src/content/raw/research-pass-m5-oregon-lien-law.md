## 2) Topic facts with citation anchors (confirmed facts vs inference)

### Confirmed facts (directly supported)

1. The PSI content outline explicitly includes **"Oregon Lien Law" with 5 scored items**. **[T1-1]**
2. Oregon lien law is codified in **ORS 87.001 to 87.093** (Construction Lien Law), including notice, perfection, foreclosure, and owner-notice provisions. **[T1-2]**
3. A person generally must provide a **Notice of Right to a Lien** to the owner under ORS 87.021 (with statutory exceptions and scope limits), and the statutory form appears in ORS 87.023. **[T1-2]**
4. A lien claim is perfected by filing under ORS 87.035, generally within **75 days** under the conditions specified in that section. **[T1-2]**
5. After filing a claim of lien, the claimant must mail notice of filing (with a copy of claim attached) to owner and mortgagee within **20 days** under ORS 87.039. **[T1-2]**
6. A construction lien does not bind longer than **120 days after filing** unless suit is brought as required by ORS 87.055 (subject to stated extended-payment language/limits). **[T1-2]**
7. A person intending to foreclose a lien must give a **10-day notice of intent to foreclose** under ORS 87.057. **[T1-2]**
8. ORS 87.093 requires original contractors to deliver the **Information Notice to Owner** on covered residential contracts and provides consequences for failure (including loss of lien right for that contract and board discipline/civil penalty authority). **[T1-2], [T1-5]**
9. OAR 812-001-0200 confirms the CCB-adopted form titled **"Information Notice to Owner About Construction Liens."** **[T1-4]**
10. Under ORS 701.131, a contractor generally may not perfect a construction lien (or pursue listed remedies) unless properly licensed/endorsed during required periods, subject to statutory exceptions. **[T1-3]**

### Inference (synthesis, not verbatim)

1. The exam likely emphasizes **timeline sequencing** (notice -> perfection -> post-filing notice -> foreclosure deadline) because the statutes impose multiple independent timing triggers. **Inference from [T1-1], [T1-2].**
2. A high-risk test area is **residential notice compliance by original contractors** because ORS 87.093 combines procedural requirements with strong consequences (potential inability to lien + discipline exposure). **Inference from [T1-2], [T1-5], [T1-4].**
3. "Lien law" questions are likely integrated with licensing compliance scenarios because ORS 701.131 conditions lien/remedy rights on licensure status. **Inference from [T1-1], [T1-3].**

4. That learners should treat the CCB/ORS/OAR text as controlling over secondary summaries. **[T1-2], [T1-4], [T2-2]**

### Should NOT assert (without tighter source support)

1. Exact enforcement outcomes, penalty amounts, or settlement likelihood for specific hypothetical fact patterns unless tied to current rule text and date-stamped authority.
2. Absolute statements that "any unpaid sub always has a lien right" without statutory conditions/exceptions (residential/commercial distinctions and notice prerequisites matter).
3. Unpublished PSI subtopic blueprint details beyond what the bulletin explicitly discloses. **[T1-1]**
4. Legal advice framing (e.g., litigation strategy predictions); keep instruction educational and statute-grounded.
