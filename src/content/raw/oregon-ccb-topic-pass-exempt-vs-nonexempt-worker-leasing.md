## 2) Topic facts with citation anchors

### Confirmed facts (directly supported by Tier 1/2 sources)

1. The Oregon CCB exam includes **Hiring and Managing Employees** as a scored subject area, which makes exempt/nonexempt employment status exam-relevant. **[T1-1]**
2. Under ORS 701.035, a CCB applicant must qualify as an independent contractor under ORS 670.600, and CCB licensing classifies contractors as **exempt** or **nonexempt**. **[T1-2], [T1-5]**
3. ORS 701.035 identifies worker-leasing usage as a nonexempt trigger and requires reapplication into the correct class if an exempt licensee adds employees or leased workers. **[T1-2]**
4. OAR 812-003-0250 operationalizes those classes by entity type and repeats that having employees or workers supplied by a worker leasing company generally places the entity in nonexempt class. **[T1-6]**
5. OAR 812-003-0321 requires filing a Notification of Changed Contractor License Status within 10 days after a change between exempt and nonexempt status. **[T1-7]**
6. ORS 701.098 includes discipline grounds for hiring employees while licensed exempt and for using worker-leasing workers while licensed exempt; it also supports immediate suspension risk for serious danger findings. **[T1-3]**
7. ORS 701.046 includes required employment/tax/workers’ comp account information in license applications where applicable, linking license records to employment status representations. **[T1-4]**
8. The PSI bulletin states the exam is open-book using the specified NASCLA Oregon manual and that questions cover laws and business practices. **[T1-1]**

### Inference (reasonable synthesis; not directly stated as one sentence in a source)

1. Because employee-management is explicitly tested and exempt/nonexempt status is directly tied to worker usage, this topic is likely assessed through practical compliance scenarios (classification + action required) rather than pure definitions. **Inference from [T1-1], [T1-2], [T1-6], [T1-7].**
2. The highest learning risk area is the transition moment (when exempt status becomes inaccurate), because multiple sources impose consequences: reclassification, notification timing, and discipline exposure. **Inference from [T1-2], [T1-3], [T1-7].**

1. **Scenario boundary ambiguity:** Public text states an exempt contractor may work with assistance linked to a nonexempt contractor/worker-leasing company in specific conditions, but does not provide enough worked examples to resolve every field scenario unambiguously. **[T1-6]**
2. **Enforcement threshold ambiguity:** ORS provides grounds and powers, but public-facing sources do not give a detailed penalty matrix by violation fact pattern for this exact topic. **[T1-3]**

## 6) Recommended assessment boundaries

### What the course **should** assess

1. Classification logic: distinguish exempt vs nonexempt status from staffing/entity facts. **[T1-2], [T1-6]**
2. Trigger recognition: identify when hiring employees or using leased workers requires status change and reapplication. **[T1-2], [T1-6]**
3. Procedural compliance: know the 10-day status-change notification requirement. **[T1-7]**
4. Consequence awareness: recognize that misclassification can trigger discipline and civil-penalty exposure. **[T1-3]**
5. Exam strategy linkage: connect this topic to the "Hiring and Managing Employees" tested domain. **[T1-1]**

### What the course **should not** assess (without stronger public support)

1. Precise penalty dollar amounts or sanction outcomes for specific fact patterns not explicitly published in current source text. **Gap noted from [T1-3].**
2. Hyper-specific edge-case interpretations of mixed staffing structures unless CCB provides published examples or formal guidance.
3. Memorization of unpublished PSI subtopic weighting beyond the public subject-area outline. **[T1-1]**
