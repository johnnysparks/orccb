6. Statewide Transit Tax (STT) is described as enacted under **HB 2017 (2017)** and codified at **ORS 320.550**; current posted withholding rate remains **0.001** while 2025 amendment/referral status is pending voter result.  
   - Citations: [oregon-dor-statewide-transit-tax@web:turn4view0-L35-L44]

7. DOR states STT is withheld from wages of Oregon residents (regardless of work location) and from nonresidents performing services in Oregon; employers are responsible for withholding/reporting/remitting.

   - Citations: [oregon-dor-statewide-transit-tax@web:turn4view0-L46-L48]

9. DOR CAT guidance states Oregon CAT applies to taxable Oregon commercial activity above **$1 million**, calculated as **$250 + 0.57%** above the threshold; return due the 15th day of the fourth month after tax-year end.  
   - Citations: [oregon-dor-corporate-activity-tax@web:turn3view0-L39], [oregon-dor-corporate-activity-tax@web:turn3view0-L108-L111]

10. DOR CAT guidance gives threshold framing: exclusion/no requirement at $750,000 or less, registration threshold at $750,000+, filing/payment threshold above $1 million.

## 3) Exact facts safe to teach

1. **Tax Basics is explicitly tested** on the CCB exam (4 scored items).
2. Employers with Oregon employees must obtain a **BIN** and run payroll-tax reporting through Oregon’s combined system.
3. A recurring exam-safe compliance fact pattern is **quarterly payroll deadlines** (Apr 30 / Jul 31 / Oct 31 / Jan 31).
4. **STT is employee-wage withholding**, not the same as employer-side TriMet/LTD payroll taxes.
5. STT currently remains at **0.001** per Oregon DOR’s posted status language as of **April 12, 2026**, with legislative-change language noted as pending election outcomes.
6. CAT is a separate business tax regime with a threshold/rate structure of **$250 + 0.57% above $1 million taxable Oregon commercial activity**.
7. Worker classification impacts payroll-tax handling; contractors should not treat employee/independent-contractor status casually.
