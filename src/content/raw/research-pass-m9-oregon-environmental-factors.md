## 2) Claim list with citation anchors (confirmed vs inference)

### Confirmed facts (safe, source-backed)

1. The Oregon CCB exam content outline includes **Environmental Factors = 4 scored items**. **[S1]**
2. The same PSI bulletin states the exam is based on the NASCLA Oregon guide and is open book. **[S1]**
3. Oregon CCB states that, for pre-1978 target housing/child-occupied facilities, contractors must complete approved training and obtain lead-based paint licensing before bidding/performing covered renovation work. **[S2]**
4. OHA states that the RRP rule requires renovators/maintenance professionals working in pre-1978 housing, schools, or child care facilities to be trained/certified and to follow specific lead-safe work practices. **[S3]**
5. OHA states that in Oregon, CCB issues the annual license to contractors that completed RRP training, while OHA administers portions of the rule for non-contractor entities (e.g., schools/child care, landlords/property managers). **[S3]**
6. DEQ states there is no known safe level of asbestos exposure and that DEQ regulates asbestos handling/removal/disposal in Oregon. **[S4]**
7. DEQ identifies OAR Chapter 340, Division 248 as the comprehensive asbestos rule set. **[S4], [S6]**
8. DEQ states asbestos abatement licensing/certification requires training, documentation of prior experience, and registration with CCB and the Oregon Business Registry. **[S5]**
9. CCB’s lead-law compilation shows ORS 701.510/701.515 structure: contractors may not perform lead-based paint renovation without certified licensing, and CCB must establish the licensing system by rule. **[S7], [S8]**
10. CCB’s lead-law compilation identifies OAR chapter 812, division 007 as the CCB lead-based paint licensing rule framework, tied to ORS 701.505–701.520 / 701.995 authorities. **[S7]**

### Inference (not direct blueprint language)

1. M9 “Environmental Factors” is most defensibly taught as **regulatory awareness + jobsite decision awareness** (lead/asbestos triggers, when specialty licensing applies, which Oregon agency has authority), not as advanced environmental engineering design. **Inference from [S1]–[S7].**
2. The highest exam-value environmental content is likely recognition-level compliance triggers (pre-1978 context, certifications/licensing, agency roles), because PSI allocates only 4 scored items for this domain. **Inference from [S1] and supporting enforcement/licensing pages [S2]–[S6].**
