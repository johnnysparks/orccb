## 2) Claim list with citation anchors

1. Oregon law generally requires a current CCB license and proper endorsement to offer, bid, or perform work as a contractor unless an exemption applies. **[S1][S3]**
2. A contractor may not hire another contractor unless the hired party is licensed and properly endorsed for the work, or exempt under ORS 701.010. **[S2][S3]**
3. Landscaping work has a separate cross-reference in ORS 701.026 to ORS 671.560/671.540 licensing context. **[S2]**
4. Independent-contractor status under ORS 670.600 includes freedom from direction/control, independently established business criteria, and chapter-671/701 licensing when required. **[S4]**
5. The PSI Oregon CCB exam currently allocates **2 scored items** to “Working with Subcontractors” out of 80 scored items total. **[S5]**
6. The PSI bulletin states the exam is open-book and uses the Oregon NASCLA guide as its reference. **[S5][S9]**
7. Official CCB contractor guidance encourages monitoring subcontractor license status (eWatch) and indicates subcontractor lien-notice relevance in required forms guidance. **[S6]**
8. CCB’s Laws & Rules page identifies ORS Chapter 701, ORS 670.600, and OAR Chapter 812 as governing references for contractors. **[S8]**

## 3) Exact facts safe to teach (high-confidence extraction)

- **Licensing baseline:** Except for exemptions, doing contractor work (including offering/bidding) requires current CCB licensure and appropriate endorsement. **[S1][S3]**
- **Subcontractor hiring rule:** A licensed contractor cannot hire another contractor for work unless that party is licensed/properly endorsed (or exempt). **[S2][S3]**
- **Exam weighting fact:** “Working with Subcontractors” is a small but explicit exam category (2 of 80 scored questions) in the current PSI bulletin version reviewed (effective 2023-02-03; accessed 2026-04-12). **[S5]**
- **Study material fact:** The Oregon NASCLA guide is the named reference for question development in PSI’s bulletin. **[S5][S9]**
- **Operational compliance cue:** CCB’s own contractor-tools page recommends active monitoring of subcontractor license status (eWatch), reinforcing practical compliance behavior. **[S6]**
