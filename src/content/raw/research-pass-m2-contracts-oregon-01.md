## 2) Claim list with citation anchors (confirmed facts vs inference)

### Confirmed facts (directly supportable from listed sources)

1. PSI identifies the Oregon contractor exam as **80 scored items, 180 minutes, 70% correct to pass**, plus non-scored items; the test is **open book**. **[T1-PSI-CIB-729-1]**
2. PSI allocates **9 scored items** to the **Contracts** domain. **[T1-PSI-CIB-729-1]**
3. Under ORS 701.305(1), a written contract is required for covered residential work if aggregate contract price exceeds **$2,000**; if price begins below and later exceeds that amount, contractor must mail/deliver written contract within **five days** after contractor knows or should know threshold is exceeded. **[T1-ORS-701-305]**
4. ORS 701.305 states failure to have the required written contract does **not automatically void** the contract. **[T1-ORS-701-305]**
5. ORS 701.310(1) gives a property owner cancellation right until **midnight of the next business day** for an initial written residential contract; ORS 701.310(2) lists exceptions (agreed early start, substantial beginning, post-period modifications). **[T1-ORS-701-310]**
6. ORS 701.315 bars contract terms that limit a person’s right to file a CCB complaint; mediation/arbitration provisions may still be included. **[T1-ORS-701-315]**
7. ORS 83.720 grants a buyer cancellation right until **midnight of the third business day** for qualifying home solicitation sales, with emergency/waiver exceptions under statutory conditions. **[T1-ORS-83-720]**
8. ORS 83.730 requires home solicitation sales to be evidenced by a signed written agreement and include prescribed cancellation-right disclosures/content. **[T1-ORS-83-730]**
9. CCB Contractor Tools states required written contract terms (contractor identity incl. CCB number, owner/worksite identification, description/price/payment terms, owner rights, mediation/arbitration disclosure if present). **[T1-CCB-CONTRACTOR-TOOLS]**
10. CCB Contractor Tools and the CCB Notices Chart state that when qualifying residential contract total exceeds $2,000, required notices must be provided and proof of delivery retained for **two years**. **[T1-CCB-CONTRACTOR-TOOLS], [T1-CCB-NOTICES-CHART]**
11. CCB Contractor Tools warns that failure to provide written contract and required consumer notices can affect lien rights (citing ORS 87.037 on the page). **[T1-CCB-CONTRACTOR-TOOLS]**

### Inference / synthesis (not a single-source quote)

1. The M2 Contracts domain should be taught as a **compliance-heavy topic** (not just common-law formation theory), because PSI weighting and CCB/ORS sources emphasize written-contract triggers, notices, and cancellation mechanics. **Inference from [T1-PSI-CIB-729-1], [T1-ORS-701-305], [T1-ORS-701-310], [T1-CCB-CONTRACTOR-TOOLS].**
2. A likely exam trap is distinguishing **next-business-day cancellation (ORS 701.310)** from **third-business-day cancellation (ORS 83.720)** based on scenario type and solicitation context. **Inference from [T1-ORS-701-310], [T1-ORS-83-720], [T1-CCB-CONTRACTOR-TOOLS].**
3. For course-safe wording, statutory numbers/time windows can be taught directly, while deeper enforceability outcomes (damages, litigation strategy, arbitration effects) should be treated as out-of-scope unless tied to additional controlling authority. **Inference from [T1-ORS-701-315], [T2-CCB-CONTRACT-LAW-RULES-2014].**

## 3) Exact facts safe to teach (high-confidence, citation-required)

1. “On the Oregon CCB exam, the Contracts domain is 9 scored questions.” **[T1-PSI-CIB-729-1]**
2. “For covered residential construction/improvement/repair, a written contract is required once aggregate contract price exceeds $2,000.” **[T1-ORS-701-305]**
3. “If a project started below $2,000 but later exceeds it, the written contract must be delivered within five days after the contractor knows or should know the threshold is exceeded.” **[T1-ORS-701-305]**
4. “For an initial written residential contract, the owner generally has until midnight of the next business day to cancel, subject to statutory exceptions.” **[T1-ORS-701-310]**
5. “Home solicitation sales have a separate three-business-day cancellation framework, with specific statutory prerequisites and exceptions.” **[T1-ORS-83-720], [T1-ORS-83-730]**
6. “Residential contracts cannot lawfully include a term that limits the right to file a CCB complaint, although mediation/arbitration provisions may be included.” **[T1-ORS-701-315]**
7. “For qualifying residential contracts over $2,000, CCB-required consumer notices must be delivered and proof retained for two years.” **[T1-CCB-CONTRACTOR-TOOLS], [T1-CCB-NOTICES-CHART]**

## 4) Unresolved ambiguities / research gaps

1. **Current OAR text granularity:** CCB pages and notice chart cite OAR sections, but this pass did not capture full current Oregon Secretary of State OAR text snapshots for each cited rule section; exact rule-language teaching should wait for direct OAR pull.
2. **Boundary cases for ORS 83 applicability:** The distinction between a qualifying home solicitation sale and a non-qualifying transaction can be fact-sensitive; edge scenarios (hybrid remote/in-person acceptance, place-of-business issues) need scenario-level legal sourcing.
3. **Remedies/penalties depth:** CCB guidance references consequences (civil penalties, potential lien impacts), but this pass did not fully map each consequence to complete statutory/regulatory enforcement pathways for exam-safe “always/never” statements.
4. **PSI subtopic weighting unknown:** PSI publishes the 9-item Contracts domain total, but no public micro-breakdown of which contract subtopics dominate within those 9 items.

- Emphasize scenario classification: residential written-contract threshold vs home-solicitation cancellation framework.
- Keep compliance framing tied to official CCB implementation pages and ORS text.

- Do not assert comprehensive OAR clause details unless current SOS rule text is directly pulled and pinned.
- Do not claim universal lien-loss outcomes without directly tying to current statutory/rule prerequisites for each fact pattern.
- Do not imply PSI publishes a detailed contracts sub-blueprint beyond the 9-item domain count.
