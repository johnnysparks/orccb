## 2) Claim list with citation anchors

### Confirmed facts (direct support)

1. PSI’s current Oregon Construction Contractors outline lists **Jobsite Safety = 4 scored items** (with exam total listed as 80 scored items, 180 minutes, 70% to pass, plus 5–8 non-scored items / 20 minutes). **[T1-1]**
2. ORS 654.010 states employers must furnish employment and a place of employment that are safe and healthful and do what is reasonably necessary to protect employee life/safety/health. **[T1-2]**
3. ORS 654.022 requires employers, owners, employees, and other persons to obey and comply with safety and health orders/rules/standards issued in scope of ORS 654 authorities. **[T1-2]**
4. In Oregon construction fall protection, federal 1926.501 “Duty to have fall protection” is repealed in Oregon and **OAR 437-003-1501 applies**. **[T1-4]**
5. OAR 437-003-0503 requires employers to provide a training program for each employee who might be exposed to fall hazards. **[T1-4]**
6. OAR 437-003-0134 requires a PPE hazard assessment and requires PPE be provided/used/maintained in sanitary and reliable condition; defective PPE must not be used; training topics are specifically enumerated. **[T1-5]**
7. 1926.20(b) (as adopted in Division 3/Subdivision C) places accident-prevention responsibility on the employer, including frequent and regular inspections of jobsites/materials/equipment by competent persons. **[T1-6]**
8. Division 3 Subdivision P states trench excavations 4 feet or more deep require a safe means of egress such that no more than 25 feet of lateral travel is needed. **[T1-7]**
9. Division 3 Subdivision P requires daily inspections of excavations/adjacent areas/protective systems by a competent person, including before work and as needed during shift; exposed employees must be removed if hazards are identified. **[T1-7]**
10. In excavation rules, protective systems are generally required except where excavation is less than 5 feet deep and a competent person finds no cave-in indication. **[T1-7]**
11. Oregon excavation rule 437-003-0096 requires estimated utility location before opening excavation and requires exact location determination by safe means when operations approach utilities; references Oregon utility-dig law cross-cites. **[T1-7]**
12. Ladder rules include removing/tagging defective ladders from service and requiring portable ladder side rails to extend at least 3 feet above upper landing (or be secured with equivalent grasping aid when extension not possible). **[T1-8]**
13. CCB’s Laws & Rules page points contractors to ORS Chapter 701 and OAR Chapter 812 while also surfacing related laws such as workers’ compensation, emphasizing multi-authority compliance context for contractors. **[T1-9]**

### Inference (explicit synthesis)

1. Because Jobsite Safety is only 4 scored blueprint items, exam questions will likely test **high-frequency compliance triggers** (fall protection, PPE/training, excavation/competent-person duties, ladder basics) rather than exhaustive technical edge cases from every subpart. **Inference from [T1-1], [T1-4], [T1-5], [T1-7], [T1-8].**
2. The best teaching frame for M9 is likely “who must do what, when, and with what documentation/controls” because statutory duty language (ORS 654) and rule text repeatedly attach duties to employers and competent persons. **Inference from [T1-2], [T1-6], [T1-7].**
3. Tier 2 hazard-trend materials can help prioritize memory hooks (e.g., fall/electrical/struck-by/caught-in patterns) but should never be treated as Oregon legal requirements unless mirrored in ORS/OAR text. **Inference from [T1-*] and [T2-1].**

## 3) Exact facts safe to teach

1. “PSI’s current Oregon CCB exam blueprint lists Jobsite Safety as 4 scored items.” **[T1-1]**
2. “ORS 654 imposes a broad duty to maintain a safe and healthful place of employment and to comply with Oregon safety orders and rules.” **[T1-2]**
3. “In Oregon construction, fall-protection duty references are Oregon-specific in OAR 437-003-1501 rather than federal 1926.501.” **[T1-4]**
4. “Employers must train employees exposed to fall hazards under OAR 437-003-0503.” **[T1-4]**
5. “Employers must assess PPE hazards, ensure correct PPE use/condition, and train workers on PPE use and limits under OAR 437-003-0134.” **[T1-5]**
6. “Construction employers must run accident-prevention programs and perform frequent and regular competent-person inspections of jobsites/materials/equipment under 1926.20(b) as adopted in Oregon.” **[T1-6]**
7. “Trench egress is required at 4 feet depth or more, and excavation inspections must be conducted by a competent person daily and after hazard-increasing events.” **[T1-7]**
8. “Defective ladders must be removed from service/tagged, and portable ladder side rails must extend 3 feet above landing unless secured with alternate grasping support.” **[T1-8]**

1. **Blueprint granularity gap:** PSI provides item counts by broad domain, but no public official subtopic weighting inside “Jobsite Safety” (e.g., fall vs. excavation vs. ladder share). **[T1-1]**
2. **Version drift risk in rule PDFs:** Division-specific PDFs carry administrative-order history and can be amended; course claims with thresholds/details should be date-checked at update time. **[T1-3], [T1-4], [T1-5], [T1-7], [T1-8]**
3. **Scope boundary gap for CCB exam prep:** CCB pages link many related law sets (e.g., workers’ comp), but exam-specific safety depth within those cross-links is not explicitly mapped publicly. **[T1-9], [T1-1]**
4. **Potential Oregon/federal wording confusion for learners:** some provisions are federally numbered but Oregon-adopted/modified; instructional content should label “Oregon applies X / did not adopt Y” where shown. **[T1-4], [T1-6], [T1-7]**
