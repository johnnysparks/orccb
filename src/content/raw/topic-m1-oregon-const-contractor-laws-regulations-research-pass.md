## 2) Topic facts with citation anchors

### Confirmed facts (directly supported)

1. The PSI bulletin lists **"Oregon Const. Contractor Laws and Regulations" as 18 items** in the scored outline for Oregon Construction Contractors. **[T1-1]**
2. The same bulletin states **80 scored items, 180 minutes, 70% to pass**, plus **5–8 non-scored items / 20 minutes**. **[T1-1]**
3. The PSI bulletin identifies the exam as **open book** and names the permitted reference (NASCLA Oregon guide) for test center use. **[T1-1]**
4. Oregon law generally requires licensing for compensated construction activity involving improvements to real property (as reflected in CCB's licensing summary, tied to ORS chapter 701). **[T2-1], [T1-2]**
5. OAR 812-003-0120 requires a current valid license to advertise as a contractor and requires license-number display in specified advertising/contracts media (with listed exceptions such as promotional gifts). **[T1-3]**
6. OAR 812-012-0110 specifies required terms when a written contract is required under ORS 701.305 (including contractor identification, job description, and price/payment terms). **[T1-4]**
7. OAR 812-012-0130 requires delivery timing and proof retention for consumer notices when ORS 701.305 written-contract requirements apply. **[T1-4]**
8. OAR 812-003-0142 sets current CCB license application fee structure, including the July 1, 2025 $400 new-license application amount. **[T1-3]**
9. OAR 812-003-0200 sets general liability insurance certification/coverage requirements and ties required amounts to OAR 812-003-0221. **[T1-3]**
10. ORS Chapter 701 contains statutory sections directly connected to M1-style issues such as definitions (701.005), license requirement (701.021), subcontractor licensing (701.026), responsible managing individual (701.091), and sanctions (701.992). **[T1-2]**

### Inference (explicit synthesis, not a direct single-source sentence)

1. M1 likely tests not only legal definitions but also **application/compliance scenarios** (e.g., ad content, contract elements, notice timing, and licensing status), because PSI allocates significant item weight and OAR provisions are operational. **Inference from [T1-1], [T1-3], [T1-4].**
2. Course learners are likely to miss questions at the **statute-rule interface** (e.g., ORS 701.305 obligations operationalized via OAR 812-012-0110/0130), so instruction should pair "what the law requires" with "what documentation proves compliance." **Inference from [T1-2], [T1-4].**

## 4) Unresolved ambiguities (public-source gaps)

1. **M1 subtopic granularity gap:** PSI publishes domain-level counts but not an official subtopic blueprint specifically for "Laws and Regulations" (no public weighting split among licensing, contracts, ads, discipline, etc.). **[T1-1]**
2. **Penalty-outcome precision gap:** ORS/OAR identify authority and sanction pathways, but public-facing sources do not provide a simple, comprehensive matrix mapping each fact pattern to expected penalty amount/outcome for exam-style scenarios. **[T1-2], [T1-3], [T1-4]**
3. **Contract-edge-case gap:** Rules clearly list required terms/notices, but public sources are limited on edge-case examples (e.g., mixed scope changes, addenda timing, multi-party sign-off patterns). **[T1-4]**
4. **Frequent update risk:** Fees/rule text can be amended; static course claims about dollar amounts or procedural specifics may drift unless update checks are scheduled. **[T1-3], [T2-2]**

## 6) Recommended boundaries for what the course should and should not assert

### What the course **should** assert

1. Published PSI exam-structure facts exactly as shown in the bulletin. **[T1-1]**
2. That Oregon contractor legal compliance is statute-plus-rule driven (ORS chapter 701 + OAR chapter 812). **[T1-2], [T1-3], [T1-4]**
3. Practical compliance fundamentals directly in rule text: licensing to advertise, license-number display, written contract minimums, and consumer notice handling. **[T1-3], [T1-4]**
4. Time-sensitive operational facts (like fees) only with explicit effective-date framing. **[T1-3]**

### What the course **should not** assert (without stronger sourcing)

1. Exact "probability" that a particular statute/rule will appear on the exam (PSI does not publish that level of blueprint detail). **Gap from [T1-1].**
2. A universal penalty schedule by violation scenario unless directly cited to a currently effective rule/statute section with precise amounts. **Gap from [T1-2], [T1-3], [T1-4].**
3. Hard claims that a fee, form, or procedural step is "permanent"; these should be framed as "as of [effective date/source]." **[T1-3], [T2-2]**
4. Interpretive legal advice for edge cases not directly resolved by cited ORS/OAR text.
