## Summary

Environmental Factors is a smaller exam domain, with PSI allocating 4 scored items in the Oregon CCB exam blueprint. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

For this domain, exam-ready knowledge is mostly regulatory recognition: identifying hazard triggers, knowing which agency has authority, and confirming licensing/certification requirements before work starts. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, oregon-ccb-specialty-licenses-lead-based-paint, oha-lead-contractors-and-painters, deq-asbestos-information.

The most testable Oregon split is lead-based paint compliance (CCB/OHA roles) versus asbestos compliance (DEQ authority and OAR chapter 340, division 248). Source: oha-lead-contractors-and-painters, deq-asbestos-information, oar-340-248.

## Lesson

### Lead-based paint in pre-1978 renovation contexts

Oregon CCB states that for covered work involving pre-1978 target housing or child-occupied facilities, contractors must complete approved lead training and obtain appropriate lead-based paint licensing before bidding or performing covered renovation activities. See lead-based paint renovation <!-- TODO: link lead-based-paint-renovation -->. Source: oregon-ccb-specialty-licenses-lead-based-paint.

OHA's lead program materials explain that renovators and related maintenance professionals in covered pre-1978 settings must use certified training pathways and lead-safe work requirements under Oregon's RRP structure. See lead-safe work practices <!-- TODO: link lead-safe-work-practices -->. Source: oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

CCB's lead-law compilation and ORS chapter 701 references identify statutory and rule framework anchors for contractor licensing in this area. Source: ccb-lbp-relevant-laws-pdf, ors-chapter-701.

### Asbestos authority and rule location

DEQ states there is no known safe level of asbestos exposure and identifies DEQ as the regulating authority for asbestos handling, removal, and disposal in Oregon. See asbestos compliance <!-- TODO: link asbestos-compliance -->. Source: deq-asbestos-information.

DEQ identifies OAR chapter 340, division 248 as the main asbestos rule set, and DEQ contractor/business guidance describes licensing or certification prerequisites, training expectations, and registration requirements for asbestos abatement pathways. Source: deq-asbestos-information, deq-asbestos-for-businesses, oar-340-248.

### Practical exam framing

Because this domain has limited item count, exam questions are more likely to test agency/rule recognition and trigger identification than technical abatement procedure details. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

## Audio Script

<!-- Estimated listening time: 6 minutes -->

Environmental factors questions on the Oregon CCB exam usually test whether you know when a hazard triggers a special compliance pathway.

For lead-based paint, think pre-1978 housing and child-occupied facilities. In Oregon, contractor licensing and lead program duties are shared across CCB and OHA depending on role and entity type.

For asbestos, think DEQ authority and Oregon's asbestos rule location in OAR chapter 340, division 248. If material disturbance may involve asbestos, your first step is confirming whether licensing, certification, and registration requirements apply before the work begins.

On test day, don't overcomplicate this domain. Identify the hazard, identify the correct agency, then identify the rule family.

## Key Terms

**Lead-based paint renovation** — Covered renovation work in pre-1978 target housing or child-occupied facilities that triggers Oregon lead training/certification and contractor licensing compliance requirements. Source: oregon-ccb-specialty-licenses-lead-based-paint, oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

**Asbestos compliance** — Oregon requirements governing asbestos-related handling or abatement activities under DEQ authority and OAR chapter 340, division 248. Source: deq-asbestos-information, oar-340-248.

**Lead-safe work practices** — Work-practice controls required in Oregon's lead renovation program for covered activities in older housing and child-occupied settings. Source: oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

## Common Mistakes

Assuming one agency regulates all environmental hazards for contractors. Oregon splits authority across CCB, OHA, and DEQ by hazard and work type. Source: oha-lead-contractors-and-painters, deq-asbestos-information, ccb-lbp-relevant-laws-pdf.

Treating pre-1978 lead requirements as optional disclosure items. Covered work requires training/certification and appropriate licensing pathways before performance. Source: oregon-ccb-specialty-licenses-lead-based-paint, oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

Assuming asbestos rules are only federal guidance. Oregon uses DEQ-administered requirements in OAR 340-248. Source: deq-asbestos-information, oar-340-248.
