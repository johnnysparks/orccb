## Summary

Environmental Factors is a smaller but high-impact Oregon CCB exam domain: PSI allocates 4 scored items to this area. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

For exam purposes, this domain is mainly about recognizing hazard-regulation triggers and agency authority rather than memorizing field procedures. Oregon lead requirements focus on pre-1978 target housing and child-occupied facilities, while asbestos requirements are administered through DEQ and OAR chapter 340, division 248. Source: oregon-ccb-specialty-licenses-lead-based-paint, oha-lead-contractors-and-painters, deq-asbestos-information, oar-340-248.

## Lesson

### Exam scope and weighting

PSI's Oregon CCB bulletin lists Environmental Factors as a separate domain with 4 scored items, so questions are typically recognition-level compliance questions in an open-book setting. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

### Lead-based paint: when the rules are triggered

Oregon CCB and OHA both describe lead-safe renovation requirements tied to pre-1978 target housing and child-occupied facilities. Contractors performing covered renovation work in those settings must complete approved training/certification pathways and hold the appropriate annual lead-based paint license through CCB before bidding or performing covered work. Source: oregon-ccb-specialty-licenses-lead-based-paint, oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

### Oregon lead program agency split

OHA states that Oregon's lead program splits responsibilities: CCB handles contractor license issuance for eligible firms, while OHA administers major program elements and lead-rule functions for non-contractor segments such as schools, child care facilities, landlords, and property managers. Source: oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

CCB's lead-law compilation also anchors the licensing framework in ORS 701.505 to 701.520 and related OAR authorities, reinforcing that the lead pathway is statute-and-rule based rather than optional guidance. Source: ccb-lbp-relevant-laws-pdf, ors-chapter-701.

### Asbestos authority and rule location

DEQ states that there is no known safe level of asbestos exposure and that DEQ regulates asbestos handling, removal, and disposal in Oregon. Source: deq-asbestos-information.

DEQ identifies OAR chapter 340, division 248 as Oregon's core asbestos requirements, and DEQ contractor/business guidance states that asbestos abatement licensing and certification require training, documented prior experience, and business registration prerequisites that include CCB registration. Source: deq-asbestos-information, oar-340-248, deq-asbestos-for-businesses.

## Audio Script

In Environmental Factors, think "trigger recognition" first.

PSI gives this domain four scored questions, so you are most likely to see short scenarios about when lead or asbestos rules apply and which agency controls the requirement. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

For lead, the recurring trigger is pre-1978 target housing or child-occupied facilities. In those settings, covered renovation work requires the right training and certification path plus the proper CCB lead-based paint licensing status before work is performed. Source: oregon-ccb-specialty-licenses-lead-based-paint, oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

Oregon splits lead responsibilities across agencies. CCB issues contractor licenses for qualified firms, while OHA administers core lead-rule functions and non-contractor program segments. Source: oha-lead-contractors-and-painters.

For asbestos, remember DEQ is the primary state authority. DEQ says there is no known safe asbestos exposure level and points contractors to OAR 340-248 for the rule text. Source: deq-asbestos-information, oar-340-248.

If a question asks where to look up asbestos requirements, your first stop is DEQ and OAR 340-248. If it asks about lead renovation licensing for contractors, use CCB and OHA lead pages together.

## Key Terms

**Lead-Based Paint Licensing** — Oregon contractor licensing pathway for covered lead renovation work, especially in pre-1978 target housing and child-occupied facilities.

**RRP Rule** — The Renovation, Repair and Painting framework requiring trained and certified renovators using lead-safe work practices in covered settings.

**Asbestos Compliance** — Oregon asbestos compliance framework administered by DEQ and codified in OAR chapter 340, division 248.

## Common Mistakes

Assuming environmental questions require detailed abatement procedure memorization. For this exam domain size, the safer focus is trigger-and-authority recognition grounded in PSI's published outline. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

Treating lead regulation as a single-agency program. Oregon lead responsibilities are split, and questions may test whether you can distinguish CCB contractor licensing from OHA administration roles. Source: oha-lead-contractors-and-painters, ccb-lbp-relevant-laws-pdf.

Confusing asbestos authority with CCB-only oversight. DEQ is the primary asbestos authority, and OAR 340-248 is the core rule location. Source: deq-asbestos-information, oar-340-248.
