## Definition

**Endorsement selection** is the process of choosing the CCB license endorsement(s) and classification alignment that match the work you intend to bid and perform. Source: oar-812-003-0200, oar-812-003-0221.

## Why it matters

Wrong endorsement choices can block legal bidding scope, trigger rework in licensing paperwork, or create compliance risk when your actual scope exceeds your declared path.

## Where it appears in a real project

It appears at estimating, proposal writing, subcontract strategy, and any cross-trade expansion decision.

## Inputs needed

- Target service list (new construction, remodel, specialty work).
- Self-perform vs subcontract model.
- Growth plan for next 12-24 months.
- Current licensing and classification references. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

## Outputs produced

- Endorsement choice mapped to intended work.
- Scope boundaries for estimating and contracts.
- Training and staffing priorities tied to scope.

## Key decisions

- Residential focus only vs broader pathways.
- Immediate scope vs staged expansion.
- Whether to defer unfamiliar scopes until endorsements and internal controls are ready.

## Basic workflow

1. List planned revenue-producing services.
2. Compare each service to endorsement/classification framework.
3. Eliminate ambiguous scopes or add controls.
4. Select endorsement path and document assumptions.
5. Re-check at renewal or major business-pivot points.

## Risks and edge cases

- Assuming "general contractor" label automatically covers all scopes.
- Taking on fringe scopes before validating licensing fit.
- Failing to re-evaluate endorsements after adding new service lines.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland homeowners frequently request mixed-scope residential work (envelope, structural, interior finish). Clear endorsement boundaries reduce proposal and change-order conflict.

## Example scenario

A small remodel firm begins offering detached ADU framing and mechanical coordination. Before advertising, the owner maps each new scope to endorsement/classification boundaries and confirms whether additional licensing actions are needed.
