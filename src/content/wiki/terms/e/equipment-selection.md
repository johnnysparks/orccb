## Definition

**Equipment selection** is choosing specific HVAC, water-heating, and related system components that satisfy calculated demand, code constraints, and owner priorities.

## Why it matters

Selection errors create lifecycle problems: noise, comfort complaints, premature wear, and utility-cost dissatisfaction. For GCs, poor selection also drives change orders and schedule slips.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Preconstruction budgeting
- Trade bid leveling and scope comparisons
- Submittal review and procurement
- Startup and owner training

## Inputs needed

- Finalized design load assumptions
- Space/access constraints
- Service capacity limits
- Owner preferences (cost, comfort, efficiency, sound)
- Lead times and local serviceability

## Outputs produced

- Equipment schedule and model choices
- Access/clearance and install requirements
- Electrical/plumbing interface requirements
- Warranty and maintenance plan inputs

## Key decisions

1. Capacity match versus oversizing temptation.
2. Efficiency tier versus first-cost constraints.
3. Single-system simplicity versus zoned control complexity.
4. Availability and replacement-part ecosystem.

## Basic workflow

1. Confirm loads and constraints are current.
2. Create 2–3 comparable equipment pathways.
3. Evaluate total installed cost and operational implications.
4. Coordinate selected option with all affected trades.
5. Validate submittals and ordering milestones.

## Risks and edge cases

- Supply-chain substitutions that weaken performance.
- Model selection without local maintenance support.
- Ignoring owner noise sensitivity.
- Value engineering that removes critical controls/features.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland homeowners often value quiet operation, smoke-season resilience, and lower shoulder-season operating costs. Equipment choices should explicitly address these local expectations.

## Example scenario

A homeowner initially picks the lowest-bid system. The GC compares lifecycle implications and serviceability, then documents the chosen mid-tier option with better comfort control and similar install timeline.

## Checklist

- [ ] Loads and constraints reconfirmed
- [ ] Options compared on cost + performance
- [ ] Trade interfaces documented
- [ ] Lead times and substitutions managed
- [ ] Owner operation expectations aligned
