Oregon CCB licenses are issued in one of two classes — **exempt** or **nonexempt** — based on whether the contractor employs workers or obtains workers through a worker-leasing arrangement. Source: ors-chapter-701, oar-812-003-0250.

## Classification Triggers

A contractor is placed in the **nonexempt** class when they:

- Hire one or more employees directly; or
- Use workers supplied by a [[worker-leasing]] company.

A contractor who works alone without employees and without leased workers qualifies for the **exempt** class. Source: ors-chapter-701, oar-812-003-0250.

## Statutory and Rule Basis

ORS 701.035 establishes the eligibility framework for CCB licensing and links classification to the contractor's independent-contractor status under ORS 670.600. The same statute identifies worker-leasing usage as a nonexempt trigger and requires that a contractor reapply into the correct class whenever their workforce situation changes. Source: ors-chapter-701, ors-670-600.

OAR 812-003-0250 operationalizes the exempt/nonexempt distinction by entity type and confirms that having employees or workers supplied by a worker-leasing company generally places the contractor in the nonexempt class. Source: oar-812-003-0250.

License applications require employment tax account and workers' compensation account information where applicable, linking license records to the contractor's employment status representations. Source: ors-chapter-701, oar-812-003-0250.

## Consequences of Misclassification

An exempt contractor who hires employees or uses worker-leasing workers without reclassifying is subject to discipline under ORS 701.098, including civil penalties and potential suspension. Source: ors-chapter-701.

When status changes, the contractor must file a Notification of Changed Contractor License Status within **10 days**. See [[license-status-change-notice]]. Source: oar-812-003-0321.

## Exam Relevance

The Oregon CCB pre-license exam covers hiring and managing employees as a scored content area. Expect scenario questions that require identifying whether a contractor's staffing situation places them in the exempt or nonexempt class and what action they must take. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

The highest-risk moment for exam questions is the **transition point** — when an exempt contractor's situation changes because they begin using employees or leased workers. Candidates must know both the classification consequence and the 10-day notification obligation. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, oar-812-003-0321.
