Oregon CCB licensing uses two employment-status classes: **exempt** and **nonexempt**. The class matters because it controls whether a contractor is licensed to operate without employees (exempt) or with employees/worker-leasing labor (nonexempt). Source: ors-701-035, oar-812-003-0250.

To be eligible for licensing in either class, applicants must satisfy Oregon independent-contractor requirements referenced by ORS chapter 701, including ORS 670.600 criteria. Source: ors-701-035, ors-670-600.

If an exempt contractor begins hiring employees or using workers from a worker leasing company, the contractor must move to the correct license status and file the required change notice. Source: ors-701-035, oar-812-003-0321.

Operating in the wrong status can lead to CCB disciplinary action, including grounds tied to employing workers or using worker-leasing workers while licensed exempt. Source: ors-701-098.
