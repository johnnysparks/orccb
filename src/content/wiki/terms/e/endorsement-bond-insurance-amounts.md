Oregon law ties the minimum [[surety-bond]] and general liability insurance amounts directly to a contractor's endorsement class; see [[insurance-requirements]] for the insurance obligation details. Selecting the correct endorsement means accepting the corresponding financial obligations. Source: ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

### Residential endorsement amounts (ORS 701.081)

| Endorsement | Bond | Insurance |
|---|---|---|
| RGC — Residential General Contractor | $25,000 | $500,000 |
| RSC — Residential Specialty Contractor | $20,000 | $300,000 |
| RLC — Residential Limited Contractor | $15,000 | $100,000 |

Source: ors-701-081.

### Commercial endorsement amounts (ORS 701.084)

| Endorsement | Bond | Insurance |
|---|---|---|
| CGC-1 — Commercial General Contractor, Tier 1 | $80,000 | $2,000,000 |
| CSC-1 — Commercial Specialty Contractor, Tier 1 | $55,000 | $1,000,000 |
| CGC-2 — Commercial General Contractor, Tier 2 | $25,000 | $1,000,000 |
| CSC-2 — Commercial Specialty Contractor, Tier 2 | $25,000 | $500,000 |

Source: ors-701-084.

### OAR alignment

OAR 812-003-0221 publishes the operational insurance amounts CCB uses when processing new license and renewal applications; these amounts align to ORS 701.073, 701.081, and 701.084. Source: oar-812-003-0221.

### CCB guide guidance

The official CCB guide for becoming a licensed contractor reinforces that applicants must submit insurance certificates and bonds matching these endorsement-specific minimums as part of application readiness. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor.

### Exam context

A common exam question type presents a contractor's endorsement class and asks which bond or insurance level applies. Knowing the representative examples in ORS 701.081 and 701.084 — and being able to locate the full tables in the open-book reference — is the primary skill tested. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.
