## Summary

Financial Management is an explicit Oregon CCB exam domain, and PSI allocates 4 scored items to this area in the candidate bulletin content outline. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

For pre-license study, the safest source-backed scope is financial compliance tied to keeping a license active: maintaining required bond and liability insurance by [[endorsement]], filing required insurance evidence, and budgeting license fees and application data requirements. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-073, ors-701-081, ors-701-084, oar-812-003-0142, oar-812-003-0200, oar-812-003-0221, oar-812-003-0260.

Because this is an open-book exam, practical success includes knowing exactly where these requirements live in ORS chapter 701 and OAR chapter 812 so you can verify amounts and rule wording during question-solving. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor.

## Lesson

### Exam context and financial-management boundaries

PSI's Oregon CCB bulletin shows Financial Management as a tested domain with 4 scored questions within the 80 scored-question exam. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

In Tier 1 Oregon sources, the clearest financial-management content concerns license-related risk financing and compliance administration, not advanced accounting theory. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-073, oar-812-003-0260.

### Bond obligations and depletion consequences

Oregon law requires applicants and renewing contractors to file and maintain a contractor license bond in endorsement-based amounts established through ORS 701.081 and ORS 701.084. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

ORS 701.068 also states that when paid claims and obligations exceed the bond's penal sum, CCB may suspend the license and may require a larger replacement bond, including an increase up to five times the normal amount as a condition of reinstatement. Source: ors-701-068.

See [[bond-requirements]] and [[bond-penal-sum]].

### Liability insurance and required certificate details

ORS 701.073 requires licensed contractors to maintain liability insurance, including products and completed operations coverage, in at least the amounts set by endorsement class. Source: ors-701-073, ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

OAR 812-003-0200 requires proof through insurance certification and specifies certificate content fields such as insurer identification, policy dates and numbers, required coverage amounts, products/completed operations language, and CCB certificate-holder information. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

OAR 812-003-0221 provides operational insurance amount schedules aligned to endorsement classes used in new and renewal licensing workflows. Source: oar-812-003-0221.

See [[insurance-requirements]] and [[insurance-certificate-requirements]].

### Fees, application data, and administrative readiness

OAR 812-003-0142 sets CCB license fee amounts and effective dates, including $400 renewal/reissue fees effective July 1, 2024, and $400 new-license applications effective July 1, 2025. Source: oar-812-003-0142.

OAR 812-003-0260 requires detailed license application data, including entity ownership and responsible-managing-individual information, making administrative completeness part of financial-management readiness for licensure. Source: oar-812-003-0260.

## Audio Script

<!-- Estimated listening time: 6 minutes -->

In Oregon CCB exam prep, Financial Management is less about advanced accounting formulas and more about whether you can keep a contractor license financially compliant.

Start with the bond. Oregon requires a contractor license bond based on your endorsement. If bond obligations are paid out past the bond's penal sum, the agency can suspend the license and require a larger bond before reinstatement.

Next, focus on insurance. Oregon requires liability insurance, including products and completed operations coverage, at endorsement-based amounts. You also need to know what an acceptable insurance certificate must contain, because rule-level details can show up in scenario questions.

Then remember fees and application administration. License fee schedules change by effective date, and applications require specific entity and responsible-managing-individual disclosures. On an open-book exam, knowing where to look these up quickly can be the difference between a correct and incorrect answer.

## Key Terms

**Financial Management** — Oregon CCB exam domain that, in Tier 1 licensing sources, is primarily evidenced through financial-compliance duties such as maintaining required bond, insurance, and fee-related licensing obligations. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, ors-701-068, ors-701-073, oar-812-003-0142.

**Bond Penal Sum** — Maximum dollar amount available under the contractor license bond before additional paid obligations can trigger CCB enforcement and potential higher-bond reinstatement conditions. Source: ors-701-068.

**Insurance Certificate Requirements** — Required rule-level fields and certification elements that must be provided to CCB as evidence of compliant contractor liability coverage. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

## Common Mistakes

Treating Financial Management as optional because it has fewer scored questions than major domains. It is still a defined scored domain in the PSI blueprint. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

Assuming bond and insurance are one-time startup items instead of ongoing licensing conditions at renewal. Oregon statutes and rules require continuing compliance. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-073, oar-812-003-0221.

Missing effective dates when reading fee tables, which can cause use of outdated amounts in planning or exam scenarios. Source: oar-812-003-0142.

Confusing insurance amount schedules with insurance certificate content rules; both are required, but they come from different OAR sections. Source: oar-812-003-0200, oar-812-003-0221.

## Open-Book Lookup Tips

- Use the PSI bulletin content outline table first to confirm Financial Management weighting and exam context. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.
- For bond duties and enforcement consequences, start in ORS 701.068 and then jump to ORS 701.081 and ORS 701.084 for endorsement amounts. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-081, ors-701-084.
