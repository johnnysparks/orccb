## Summary

Financial Management is tested as 4 scored items on the Oregon CCB exam, representing approximately 5 percent of the 80-question scored pool. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

The exam-tested scope of financial management centers on the financial compliance obligations tied to licensing: maintaining the correct [[surety-bond]] and liability insurance amounts for your endorsement class (see [[endorsement-bond-insurance-amounts]]), understanding what happens when a bond is depleted, and knowing the license fee schedule (see [[ccb-license-fees]]). Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-073, oar-812-003-0142.

Because the PSI exam is open-book and the NASCLA Oregon guide is the stated reference, exam readiness includes knowing which ORS and OAR sections to navigate quickly during the test. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, nascla-contractors-guide-or-2e.

## Lesson

### Financial compliance as a licensing obligation

A CCB license is not a one-time achievement — it must be maintained. Oregon law requires active contractors to keep a [[surety-bond]] and general liability insurance on file at all times, with amounts that vary by endorsement class. Licensing fees are also a recurring operating cost. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-073, oar-812-003-0142.

These three obligations — bond, insurance, and fees — are the core of what Financial Management tests on the Oregon CCB exam. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

### Surety bonds by endorsement

Under ORS 701.068, licensed contractors must file a surety bond with the CCB. The required penal sum depends on the contractor's endorsement class, set by ORS 701.081 for residential endorsements and ORS 701.084 for commercial endorsements. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

Representative residential bond requirements under ORS 701.081:
- **RGC** (Residential General Contractor): $25,000 bond / $500,000 insurance.
- **RSC** (Residential Specialty Contractor): $20,000 bond / $300,000 insurance.
- **RLC** (Residential Limited Contractor): $15,000 bond / $100,000 insurance.

Source: ors-701-081.

Representative commercial bond requirements under ORS 701.084:
- **CGC-1** (Commercial General Contractor, Tier 1): $80,000 bond / $2,000,000 insurance.
- **CSC-1** (Commercial Specialty Contractor, Tier 1): $55,000 bond / $1,000,000 insurance.
- **CGC-2** (Commercial General Contractor, Tier 2): $25,000 bond / $1,000,000 insurance.
- **CSC-2** (Commercial Specialty Contractor, Tier 2): $25,000 bond / $500,000 insurance.

Source: ors-701-084.

OAR 812-003-0221 publishes the operational insurance amounts CCB uses for new and renewal applications, aligning to ORS 701.073, 701.081, and 701.084. Source: oar-812-003-0221.

See [[endorsement-bond-insurance-amounts]] and [[contractor-classification]].

### Bond depletion consequences

If claims paid against a contractor's bond exceed the bond's penal sum, ORS 701.068 authorizes CCB to suspend the license. Before reinstating the license, CCB may require the contractor to file a replacement bond of up to five times the ordinary amount for that endorsement class. Source: ors-701-068.

See [[bond-depletion]] and [[bond-requirements]].

### Insurance requirements and certificates

ORS 701.073 requires licensed contractors to maintain general liability insurance — including products and completed-operations coverage — in at least the endorsement-specific minimum amounts, and to provide evidence of coverage at licensure and renewal. Source: ors-701-073.

OAR 812-003-0200 specifies the required content of the [[insurance-certificate]] submitted to CCB: the insurer's name, policy number, policy effective and expiration dates, the required minimum coverage amounts, a statement of products/completed-operations coverage, and the CCB as the named certificate holder. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

See [[insurance-requirements]] and [[insurance-certificate]].

### License fees

OAR 812-003-0142 sets the CCB license fee schedule, including phased effective dates. As of the 2024–2025 cycle, renewal and reissue fees are $400 (effective 2024-07-01) and new-license application fees are $400 (effective 2025-07-01), with a prior $325 transition amount. Source: oar-812-003-0142.

Fee effective dates are exam-testable because they affect application timing decisions. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

See [[ccb-license-fees]] and [[ccb-license]].

### Application disclosures

OAR 812-003-0260 requires applicants to submit detailed entity, ownership, and Responsible Managing Individual (RMI) information as part of the licensing application. These administrative disclosures are part of financial-compliance management for the business. Source: oar-812-003-0260.

### Open-book exam strategy

PSI identifies the NASCLA Oregon Contractors Guide, 2nd Edition, as the reference used to prepare exam questions; it is permitted during the exam. For financial management questions, fast lookup skills in the ORS chapter 701 bond and insurance tables and OAR 812-003-0142 fee schedule table are high-value. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, nascla-contractors-guide-or-2e.

## Audio Script

<!-- Estimated listening time: 5 minutes -->

Financial Management is a small but tested domain — four scored questions out of eighty on the Oregon CCB exam. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

The core idea is this: your CCB license has ongoing financial compliance obligations. Three of the most exam-tested are your surety bond, your general liability insurance, and your licensing fees. Each of these must be maintained at levels tied to your specific endorsement class. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-073, oar-812-003-0142.

On bonds: ORS 701.081 sets the amounts for residential endorsements and ORS 701.084 for commercial endorsements. For example, a Residential General Contractor needs a $25,000 bond and $500,000 in liability insurance. A Commercial General Contractor at Tier 1 needs $80,000 in bond and two million dollars in insurance. Source: ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

Bond depletion is a key scenario. If claims against your bond exceed the penal sum, CCB can suspend your license under ORS 701.068. Reinstatement may require posting a new bond up to five times the ordinary amount. Source: ors-701-068.

On insurance, ORS 701.073 requires you to carry general liability coverage including products and completed-operations, and you must provide an insurance certificate with required elements. OAR 812-003-0200 lists those elements: insurer name, policy number, dates, required amounts, products-completed-ops statement, and CCB as certificate holder. Source: ors-701-073, oar-812-003-0200.

On fees, OAR 812-003-0142 has a phased schedule. Renewal and reissue fees became $400 on July 1, 2024. New applications moved to $400 on July 1, 2025. The effective dates are important for application timing questions. Source: oar-812-003-0142.

Finally, the exam is open-book and the NASCLA Oregon guide is your reference. For financial management, know where to find the bond and insurance tables in ORS chapter 701, and the fee schedule in OAR 812-003-0142. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, nascla-contractors-guide-or-2e.

## Key Terms

**[[surety-bond]]** — A three-party financial guarantee in which a bonding company promises to pay valid claims against a licensed contractor up to the bond's penal sum, with the contractor required to file and maintain this bond as a condition of CCB licensure. Source: ors-701-068.

**[[insurance-certificate]]** — A document submitted to CCB certifying a contractor's general liability insurance coverage, which must include the insurer's name, policy number, effective and expiration dates, required coverage amounts, a products/completed-operations statement, and the CCB as named certificate holder. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

**[[ccb-license-fees]]** — The fee amounts established in OAR 812-003-0142 for new-license applications, renewals, and reissues; phased effective dates in the rule affect when a specific fee amount applies. Source: oar-812-003-0142.

**[[endorsement-bond-insurance-amounts]]** — The endorsement-specific minimum bond and liability insurance amounts set by ORS 701.081 for residential classes and ORS 701.084 for commercial classes, which contractors must carry to obtain and maintain CCB licensure. Source: ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

**[[bond-depletion]]** — A condition triggered when cumulative claims paid against a contractor's surety bond exceed the bond's penal sum, which can result in CCB license suspension and a requirement to file a replacement bond of up to five times the ordinary amount before reinstatement. Source: ors-701-068.

## Common Mistakes

Confusing bond amount with insurance amount. The two are separate financial obligations with different dollar figures, both determined by endorsement class. A surety bond is not insurance. Source: ors-701-068, ors-701-073.

Applying residential bond/insurance amounts to a commercial scenario, or vice versa. ORS 701.081 and ORS 701.084 are separate statutes with different tables. Always identify the endorsement class in the scenario first. Source: ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

Assuming bond depletion only affects future projects. Under ORS 701.068, once obligations exceed the penal sum, CCB may immediately suspend the license, not just restrict new bids. Source: ors-701-068.

Missing required fields on an insurance certificate. OAR 812-003-0200 lists specific required elements; omitting any one of them — such as failing to name CCB as certificate holder — is a compliance failure. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

Using the wrong fee for an application because of effective-date confusion. OAR 812-003-0142 has phased dates; a new application filed before 2025-07-01 used $325, not $400. Source: oar-812-003-0142.
