## Definition

**Framing and envelope coordination** is aligning structural framing decisions with insulation, air-sealing, moisture-control, and mechanical pathway needs so the enclosure performs as designed.

## Why it matters

Many project failures occur at trade interfaces, not within a single trade scope. Coordination prevents thermal bridges, moisture traps, and late rough-in conflicts.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Plan review and detail development
- Framing phase decisions on openings/chases/blocking
- Envelope installation and inspection prep
- Mechanical/electrical/plumbing rough interface resolution

## Inputs needed

- Structural framing plan
- Envelope assembly targets
- Air barrier and moisture-control strategy
- Mechanical/plumbing routing needs
- Inspection sequencing requirements

## Outputs produced

- Coordinated framing + enclosure details
- Trade interface notes and hold-point inspections
- Air/moisture continuity plan
- Reduced rework at rough and finish stages

## Key decisions

1. Where to prioritize continuous control layers.
2. How to route services with minimal envelope compromise.
3. Which details need mockups or pre-task walks.
4. Timing of hold points before concealment.

## Basic workflow

1. Review structural and enclosure intents together.
2. Identify conflict zones (penetrations, headers, chases, balconies).
3. Assign responsibilities for each control-layer transition.
4. Conduct pre-cover inspections at critical interfaces.
5. Capture as-built deviations and corrective actions.

## Risks and edge cases

- Unplanned penetrations breaking air/moisture continuity.
- Thermal bridge creation from structural substitutions.
- Incomplete transition details at openings and roof-wall junctions.
- Remodel tie-ins between new and legacy assemblies.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Southern Oregon seasonal conditions and smoke events amplify the cost of envelope leaks and poor interface details. Better coordination improves comfort, IAQ, and durability outcomes in both new builds and retrofit-heavy neighborhoods.

## Example scenario

A framing crew adds field blocking that interrupts planned duct and insulation pathways. Because the GC runs an interface walk before sheathing close-in, the team revises details and avoids envelope penetrations that would have caused future moisture risk.

## Checklist

- [ ] Interface zones identified before framing starts
- [ ] Control-layer transitions assigned by trade
- [ ] Rough routing checked against envelope details
- [ ] Critical hold-point inspections completed
- [ ] As-built interface notes archived
