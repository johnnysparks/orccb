## Definition

**Pre-license education and exam prep** is the structured learning phase where you complete required Oregon pre-license training and prepare to pass the contractor exam with rule-accurate, open-book-ready methods. Source: oregon-ccb-prelicense, psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

## Why it matters

Passing quickly reduces licensing timeline risk and keeps momentum toward lawful project startup.

## Where it appears in a real project

It appears before bidding as a licensed prime, and again when teams train new leaders to sustain compliance quality.

## Inputs needed

- Course enrollment and completion plan.
- Exam bulletin and domain weighting.
- Study calendar with scenario practice.
- Core law/rule references for lookups.

## Outputs produced

- Course completion record.
- Exam pass result.
- Reusable lookup and tabbing strategy for future compliance decisions.

## Key decisions

- Cram approach vs spaced review.
- Solo study vs guided cohort.
- Topic-first sequencing vs exam-weight-first sequencing.

## Basic workflow

1. Confirm current pre-license requirements.
2. Complete approved education provider coursework.
3. Build an exam blueprint study plan.
4. Run timed scenario practice.
5. Sit for exam and analyze weak areas if retake is needed.

## Risks and edge cases

- Studying outdated materials.
- Memorizing instead of practicing open-book navigation.
- Ignoring low-confidence domains that later become operational weak points.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland projects often combine residential scope with strict local process expectations; stronger exam prep on contract and compliance domains improves first-project execution quality.

## Example scenario

A candidate plans to open a small residential GC business in Ashland. They map study time to exam-weighted domains, build a statute lookup index, and pass on the first attempt.
