## Definition

**PV and battery readiness** is designing and documenting a home so solar and storage can be added now or later with minimal rework, permitting friction, and disruption.

## Why it matters

Many owners delay PV/storage for budget reasons but want future flexibility. Readiness planning protects optionality and avoids expensive retrofit demolition.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Electrical service and panel design
- Roof and equipment location coordination
- Conduit/pathway planning
- Owner turnover documentation

## Inputs needed

- Owner timeline for PV/storage adoption
- Service sizing and panel capacity plan
- Roof geometry and shading assumptions
- Equipment location possibilities (inverters/battery)
- Utility interconnection considerations

## Outputs produced

- Reserved electrical capacity and physical pathways
- Documented panel provisions and labeling
- Site/roof notes supporting future installation
- Homeowner readiness packet

## Key decisions

1. What to pre-install now versus leave as documented future work.
2. How much panel and conduit reserve to provide.
3. Battery location options and safety/clearance constraints.
4. Whether current budget supports partial implementation.

## Basic workflow

1. Clarify owner readiness goals and timeline.
2. Coordinate service/panel future capacity.
3. Reserve routing and physical space during rough-in.
4. Update as-built docs with future connection points.
5. Hand off readiness guidance and triggers for upgrade.

## Risks and edge cases

- Roof layout decisions limiting future module placement.
- Panel fully consumed by current circuits.
- No planned pathway from roof to electrical room.
- Homeowner unaware of readiness features at turnover.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland homeowners often value outage resilience and long-term electrification. Even when PV/storage is deferred, readiness details can preserve affordability and reduce permit/design friction later.

## Example scenario

A homeowner defers battery purchase by two years. Because the GC reserved panel capacity, conduit pathway, and equipment wall space, future installation proceeds with minimal disruption.

## Checklist

- [ ] Owner PV/storage timeline documented
- [ ] Reserve capacity and pathways coordinated
- [ ] Roof/equipment space conflicts resolved
- [ ] As-built readiness notes completed
- [ ] Owner informed of future upgrade path
