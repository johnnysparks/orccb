## Definition

**Panel schedules** are organized lists of electrical circuits, breaker sizes, connected loads, and destination areas used to coordinate design, installation, and inspection.

## Why it matters

Accurate schedules reduce field confusion, speed inspections, and make future maintenance safer. Poor schedules cause mislabeled circuits, troubleshooting delays, and callback risk.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Permit plan sets
- Electrical rough and trim phases
- Inspection and closeout documentation
- Owner turnover packets

## Inputs needed

- Final service/panel strategy
- Circuiting plan by room/equipment
- Load assumptions and dedicated-circuit needs
- Field changes captured during install

## Outputs produced

- Panel schedule tables
- Circuit labels and legends
- As-built electrical records
- Maintenance/troubleshooting reference for owner

## Key decisions

1. Level of detail to include in permit vs as-built sets.
2. Naming conventions for long-term clarity.
3. Reserved spaces/circuits for future additions.
4. How field revisions are controlled and approved.

## Basic workflow

1. Draft schedule from electrical design intent.
2. Verify with electrician before rough-in completion.
3. Update after approved field changes.
4. Align physical panel labels to schedule.
5. Deliver final as-built version at handoff.

## Risks and edge cases

- Circuit labels drift from actual installation.
- Mid-project scope changes are not reflected in documents.
- Shared circuits create nuisance trips and confusion.
- Future additions consume unplanned panel capacity.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland remodel and ADU work often evolves during construction. Strict schedule updates and as-built discipline protect owner safety and reduce post-occupancy troubleshooting time.

## Example scenario

An ADU addition adds mini-split, induction range, and EV outlet late in design. The GC requires panel schedule revision before rough closeout, preventing mislabeled breakers at final inspection.

## Checklist

- [ ] Dedicated-load circuits clearly identified

- [ ] Panel labels match documentation
- [ ] Final as-built schedule delivered to owner
