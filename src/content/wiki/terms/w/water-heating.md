## Definition

**Water heating** is the selection, sizing, and integration of systems that provide domestic hot water safely and reliably to household fixtures and appliances.

## Why it matters

Owners notice hot-water performance immediately. Poor water-heating decisions cause comfort complaints, utility surprises, and expensive retrofit work.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Early equipment strategy (tank, tankless, heat-pump options)
- Plumbing/electrical coordination
- Location, clearance, drainage, and service planning
- Startup and owner operation training

## Inputs needed

- Household demand profile and fixture count
- Fuel/electrical availability and capacity
- Space/noise constraints
- Hot-water delivery distance assumptions
- Budget and efficiency priorities

## Outputs produced

- Selected water-heating system and capacity
- Distribution and recirculation strategy
- Electrical/plumbing interface requirements
- Maintenance and operating instructions

## Key decisions

1. System type based on demand and utility context.
2. Capacity to handle peak concurrent use.
3. Recirculation or distribution strategy for wait-time control.
4. Location and sound-management considerations.

## Basic workflow

1. Estimate hot-water usage patterns.
2. Evaluate equipment pathways and utility impacts.
3. Coordinate install requirements with trades.
4. Install, commission, and verify delivery performance.
5. Train owner on settings and maintenance.

## Risks and edge cases

- Peak simultaneous demand exceeds selected capacity.
- Long runs produce unacceptable wait times.
- Electrical upgrades required late for all-electric options.
- Noise/vibration complaints in tight mechanical spaces.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland homeowners increasingly compare electrified water-heating options against gas systems. Early coordination with service sizing and panel capacity reduces late surprises.

## Example scenario

A remodel replaces gas tank water heating with electric heat-pump water heating. The GC coordinates panel capacity and placement early, avoiding schedule delay and delivering better operating cost outcomes.
