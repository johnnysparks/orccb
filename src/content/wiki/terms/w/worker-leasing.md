**Worker leasing** is an arrangement in which a contractor obtains workers through a professional employer organization (PEO) or worker-leasing company rather than hiring those workers as direct employees. Source: ors-chapter-701.

## CCB Classification Impact

Under ORS 701.035 and OAR 812-003-0250, using workers supplied by a worker-leasing company is treated the same as hiring employees for purposes of CCB license classification. A contractor who obtains workers through a leasing arrangement must hold a **nonexempt** license rather than an exempt license. Source: ors-chapter-701, oar-812-003-0250.

An exempt contractor who begins using a worker-leasing company without first reclassifying violates ORS 701.098 and is subject to discipline, including civil penalties and potential suspension. Source: ors-chapter-701.

## Status-Change Notification Obligation

When a contractor's use of leased workers triggers a change from exempt to nonexempt status, the contractor must file a Notification of Changed Contractor License Status with the CCB within **10 days** of the change. See [[license-status-change-notice]]. Source: oar-812-003-0321.

## Exam Relevance

The Oregon CCB pre-license exam covers hiring and managing employees as a scored content area. Worker leasing is a key trigger for nonexempt status, and exam scenarios frequently test whether a candidate recognizes it as functionally equivalent to direct employment for classification purposes. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

Candidates should be able to:
- Identify when a contractor's use of a leasing arrangement requires a class change.
- State the 10-day notification deadline.
- Recognize discipline exposure for failing to reclassify.

Source: ors-chapter-701, oar-812-003-0250, oar-812-003-0321.
