## Summary

Oregon treats subcontractor compliance as a licensing control, not just a project-management preference. Under ORS 701.026, a contractor generally may not hire another contractor unless that hired party is properly licensed and endorsed for the work, or is exempt under ORS 701.010. This means pre-award and pre-start license checks are part of legal compliance, not optional paperwork. Source: ors-701-026, ors-701-010.

The PSI Candidate Information Bulletin identifies "Working with Subcontractors" as a tested domain with 2 scored items out of 80 total scored items in the reviewed bulletin version. Even with a small item count, these questions are often straightforward points if you know the rule path and can verify licensure quickly during open-book testing. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

## Lesson

### Core Rule: Do Not Hire Unlicensed or Improperly Endorsed Contractors

ORS 701.021 sets the baseline that a person must hold the required CCB license and endorsement before offering, bidding, or performing contractor work unless a statutory exemption applies. ORS 701.026 applies that framework directly to subcontracting: a contractor may not hire another contractor unless the hired party is licensed and properly endorsed for the work, or exempt under ORS 701.010. Source: ors-701-021, ors-701-026, ors-701-010.

In practical terms, subcontractor selection is not complete when price and scope are agreed. You still need to confirm active license status and the correct endorsement before work starts. CCB's Contractor Tools page reinforces this operationally by directing contractors to monitor license status (including eWatch alerts). Source: oregon-ccb-contractor-tools.

See also [[subcontractor-licensing]], [[ccb-license]], and [[laws-and-regulations]].

### Exemptions and the Limits of Exemption Arguments

ORS 701.010 lists exemptions from CCB licensure. If a subcontractor candidate claims an exemption, you should treat that as a legal status question that must match statute language, not as a business preference. If no exemption applies, licensing and endorsement are still required. Source: ors-701-010, ors-701-026.

On exam questions, exemption distractors are common. The safe approach is to first ask whether ORS 701.010 clearly applies; if not, assume full licensing requirements remain in force. Source: ors-701-010, psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

### Subcontractor Compliance vs Worker Classification

Subcontractor license compliance and worker classification overlap but are not identical. ORS 670.600 defines independent contractor status and includes control and independently established business criteria, plus required licensing when the work is subject to licensing laws. So a person can still fail licensing compliance even when parties label the relationship "independent contractor." Source: ors-670-600, ors-701-021.

For study sequencing, use this topic together with [[employee-vs-subcontractor]] and [[independent-contractor]] so you can separate licensing obligations from labor/tax classification analysis. Source: ors-670-600, ors-701-026.

### Exam Weight and Open-Book Strategy

The PSI bulletin allocates 2 scored items to this domain. Because the exam is open book, speed matters: know where to verify the licensing rule (ORS 701.026), the general licensing baseline (ORS 701.021), and the exemption list (ORS 701.010). Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, ors-701-026, ors-701-021, ors-701-010.

A high-yield method is to treat each scenario as a checklist:
1. Is the hired party performing contractor work covered by ORS Chapter 701?
2. If yes, is there an active license with the proper endorsement?
3. If no license, does a specific ORS 701.010 exemption apply?

That sequence mirrors the statute structure and reduces trick-answer risk. Source: ors-701-026, ors-701-010, oregon-ccb-laws-rules.

## Audio Script

In Oregon, working with subcontractors is first a legal compliance issue and only second a scheduling issue.

The key rule is in ORS 701.026. If you are hiring another contractor to do part of your project, that hired party generally must be properly licensed and endorsed for the work. If they are not, you can only proceed when a specific exemption in ORS 701.010 applies.

Think of this as a gate. Before work starts, confirm license status and endorsement. Don't wait until after a dispute or inspection. The CCB's contractor guidance supports this workflow by encouraging active monitoring tools like eWatch.

On the exam, this domain is small — two scored questions in the current bulletin — but that makes it a good place to pick up reliable points. Memorize the rule path: ORS 701.026 for subcontractor hiring, ORS 701.021 for the general licensing baseline, and ORS 701.010 for exemptions.

Also remember that worker classification is related but separate. ORS 670.600 explains independent-contractor status. A label in a contract is not enough by itself if licensing requirements still apply.

If you use that sequence during open-book questions, you'll avoid most subcontractor trap answers.

## Key Terms

**Subcontractor Licensing** — The rule that a contractor may not hire another contractor unless that hired party is properly licensed and endorsed for the work, or exempt under ORS 701.010. Source: ors-701-026, ors-701-010.

**Endorsement** — The license category/authorization that must match the type of contractor work being offered, bid, or performed under Oregon's contractor licensing framework. Source: ors-701-021, ors-701-026.

## Common Mistakes

**Assuming a signed subcontract is enough.** A signed agreement does not replace the duty to verify active licensing and endorsement before work begins. Source: ors-701-026.

**Treating exemption claims as automatic.** Exemptions must fit ORS 701.010. If they do not, the licensing rule still applies. Source: ors-701-010, ors-701-026.

**Confusing independent-contractor language with licensing compliance.** ORS 670.600 supports classification analysis but does not erase separate CCB licensing duties under ORS Chapter 701. Source: ors-670-600, ors-701-021.
