## Definition

**Workers' comp awareness** is the practical ability to recognize when worker classification, supervision, and jobsite reality create workers' compensation obligations and risk controls. Source: ors-chapter-654.

## Why it matters

Classification mistakes are expensive and can cascade into licensing, payroll, and safety exposure.

## Where it appears in a real project

It appears when onboarding helpers, using labor-only crews, hiring subs, or changing who controls means and methods in the field.

## Inputs needed

- Worker role definitions.
- Contracting model (employee, subcontractor, leased labor).
- Safety and supervision structure.
- Insurance status and reporting practices.

## Outputs produced

- Clear classification decisions.
- Coverage alignment and documentation.
- Fewer surprise liabilities during audits or claims.

## Key decisions

- Employee vs subcontractor treatment.
- Documentation standards for each labor relationship.
- Escalation steps when scope or control changes.

## Basic workflow

1. Define labor need and supervision level.
2. Classify worker relationship using Oregon criteria.
3. Confirm workers' comp implications.
4. Document decision and supporting records.
5. Reassess when role conditions change.

## Risks and edge cases

- Calling someone a subcontractor while managing them like an employee.
- Informal cash labor arrangements without classification review.
- Role drift across a project without re-documentation.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Small Ashland crews often blend roles quickly; periodic classification reviews help avoid accidental noncompliance during peak remodeling season.

## Example scenario

A GC uses a "temporary helper" for demolition and cleanup over several weeks. As supervision and schedule control increase, the company reevaluates classification and updates coverage handling.

## Checklist

- [ ] Labor relationship documented before start.
- [ ] Classification rationale recorded.
- [ ] Coverage implications reviewed.
- [ ] Safety/supervision setup aligned.
- [ ] Reassessment trigger for scope/control changes.
