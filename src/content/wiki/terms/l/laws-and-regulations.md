## Summary

Oregon construction contractor law operates on two levels: statutes enacted by the Oregon Legislature (ORS Chapter 701) and administrative rules written by the CCB (OAR Chapter 812). The statutes set the broad legal requirements — who must be licensed, what contracts must contain, and what sanctions apply for violations. The administrative rules fill in the operational details — exact fee amounts, required advertising language, precise notice-delivery timing, and proof-retention standards.

The PSI exam blueprint allocates 18 of 80 scored items to "Oregon Const. Contractor Laws and Regulations," the largest single domain in the exam. Questions test both definitions and applied compliance scenarios: whether a contractor's advertisement is legal, whether a contract meets statutory minimums, whether a notice was delivered correctly, and whether a licensee has the required financial protections in place.

Understanding how the statute-rule layers interact is the central skill for this domain. When an exam question asks what a contractor "must" do, trace the answer to a specific ORS or OAR provision. Familiarity with which rule-section governs which compliance area is often enough to answer correctly even in an open-book format.

## Lesson

### The Two-Layer Legal Framework

Oregon contractor law is structured as a two-layer system. The **Oregon Revised Statutes (ORS)** are enacted by the Oregon Legislature and form the controlling legal authority. **Oregon Administrative Rules (OAR)** are promulgated by the CCB under authority granted by the statutes and provide the operational detail that the statutes delegate to the agency.

Key chapter references:
- **ORS Chapter 701** — Construction Contractors. This is the primary statutory authority for licensing, bonding, insurance, contracts, and sanctions.
- **OAR Chapter 812** — CCB Rules. Divided into divisions that each cover a topic area: Division 3 covers licensing operations and fees; Division 12 covers contractor duties including contract terms and consumer notices.

When a course claim or exam question cites a legal requirement, it traces to one of these layers. A rule can never override the statute it implements; if they conflict, the statute controls.

### Who Must Be Licensed

Under ORS Chapter 701, any person who performs compensated construction work that involves an improvement to real property in Oregon generally must hold a current CCB license. The license requirement applies to the business entity or sole proprietor performing the work, not merely to individual workers on the crew.

Key ORS provisions within Chapter 701:
- **ORS 701.005** — Definitions (defining "contractor," "residential structure," and related terms)
- **ORS 701.021** — License requirement for performing or offering to perform construction work for compensation
- **ORS 701.026** — Subcontractor licensing; a general contractor's obligation to use licensed subcontractors
- **ORS 701.091** — Responsible Managing Individual (RMI) requirement

The responsible managing individual <!-- TODO: link responsible-managing-individual --> is the named individual on the license who takes the qualifying examination and is responsible for ongoing compliance. A business entity's license depends on a qualified RMI remaining active and associated with the license.

### Advertising and License-Number Display

A contractor may not advertise as a licensed contractor without holding a **current, valid CCB license** <!-- TODO: link ccb-license -->. This is the core rule of OAR 812-003-0120.

Beyond that threshold requirement, the rule specifies how the license number must be displayed:
- The CCB **license number must appear** in advertisements, contracts, bids, and other specified media where the contractor promotes their construction services.
- Promotional gift items (pens, hats, etc.) are an example of a listed exception where the license-number display requirement does not apply.
- The rule covers a broad range of media: print, digital, signage, and written contracts.

Exam tip: The advertising rule is frequently tested through compliance scenarios. If a question presents an ad or a contract and asks whether it is compliant, check whether the CCB license number appears.

### Written Contract Minimum Elements

ORS 701.305 triggers a written contract requirement for residential construction work above a threshold price. OAR 812-012-0110 specifies the minimum elements the contract must contain when ORS 701.305 applies.

Required elements under this combined statute-rule framework include:
- The contractor's name, address, and **CCB license number** — a high-frequency exam topic
- A description of the work to be performed
- The contract price or the basis for calculating it
- Estimated start and substantial completion dates
- Disclosure that the contractor is bonded and insured (or the specific coverage amounts)

Failure to include required elements exposes the contractor to CCB complaints, license sanctions, and potential loss of lien rights.

For the exam, the most commonly tested required element is the **CCB license number**. When a scenario describes a written residential contract, always verify whether the license number is present.

### Consumer Notice Requirements

When ORS 701.305 written-contract requirements apply, Oregon administrative rules also require the contractor to deliver a **consumer notice** <!-- TODO: link consumer-notice --> to the residential client.

OAR 812-012-0130 governs the notice requirements:
- The notice must be delivered **before work begins**
- The contractor must retain **proof of delivery** — not merely a copy of the notice

This is a statute-rule interface question: ORS 701.305 sets the underlying obligation, and OAR 812-012-0130 specifies the operational details (timing and proof retention). Exam questions on this topic often test whether the contractor knows that documentation of delivery — not just delivery itself — is required.

### Insurance Requirements

Licensed contractors must maintain general liability insurance at the coverage amounts set by CCB rules. The insurance framework involves three linked provisions:

- **OAR 812-003-0200** — Sets the general insurance certification and compliance requirements (insurer ratings, policy conditions, and how the CCB verifies coverage)
- **OAR 812-003-0221** — Sets the required coverage amounts by endorsement level
- **ORS 701.073** — Statutory authorization for the insurance requirement; ORS 701.081 and 701.084 set the residential and commercial endorsement-level minimums

The key principle: both the bond and the insurance requirement must be continuously maintained for the license to remain active. A lapse suspends the license.

### License Application Fees

OAR 812-003-0142 establishes the CCB license fee schedule. Effective July 1, 2025, the new-license application fee is **$400**. Fee amounts are set by rule and can be amended; any course statement about dollar figures should be understood as effective as of the cited rule version.

The license fee is an operational detail that appears on the exam as a recall question. Know the current fee amount and its source in OAR.

### Sanctions

ORS Chapter 701 contains sanction authority. ORS 701.992 addresses civil penalties for violations. The CCB can suspend or revoke a license, impose civil penalties, and refer criminal violations to the appropriate authority. The specific penalty amounts and violation-to-outcome mappings are set in ORS and OAR but public sources do not provide a comprehensive matrix; study the general structure rather than memorizing every dollar amount.

For this exam, you care most about **ORS Chapter 701**, which is the main Construction Contractors statute, and **OAR Chapter 812**, which is the CCB's rule book. It covers general contractors, specialty contractors, and subcontractors. The license belongs to the business, but a specific named individual — called the **Responsible Managing Individual**, or RMI — is tied to each license. The RMI takes the qualifying exam, and the license depends on that person staying qualified and associated with the business.

Now let's talk about **advertising**. Under OAR 812-003-0120, you cannot advertise as a licensed contractor unless you actually have a current, valid CCB license. Pens and hats with your name on them are explicitly exempted — but a website, a flyer, a written contract — those need the number.

This comes up a lot on the exam. If a question shows you an advertisement or a contract and asks "is this legally compliant?" — check for the CCB number first.

Next: **written contracts**. ORS 701.305 requires a written contract for residential construction work over a threshold price. Missing required elements doesn't automatically void the contract, but it does open the contractor up to CCB complaints and potential loss of lien rights. Exam questions on this topic almost always focus on the license number — it's the most commonly tested element.

## Key Terms

**CCB License** — An active Oregon Construction Contractors Board license that a contractor must hold before performing compensated construction work or advertising as a contractor. The license number must appear in written contracts and specified advertising media.

**Responsible Managing Individual** — The individual designated on a contractor's CCB license application who is responsible for the qualifying examination, maintaining required insurance and bond, and ensuring the business complies with Oregon contractor law under ORS Chapter 701.

**Consumer Notice** — A CCB-required disclosure that must be delivered to a residential client before work begins on a project subject to ORS 701.305. OAR 812-012-0130 specifies the required delivery timing and requires proof-of-delivery to be retained.

## Common Mistakes

**Confusing ORS and OAR.** ORS is a statute passed by the Legislature; OAR is an administrative rule written by the CCB under statutory authority. Exam questions may ask which type of authority governs a specific requirement. Licensing, contract thresholds, and sanctions are primarily ORS; fee amounts, insurance certification details, and notice-delivery procedures are primarily OAR.

**Believing the advertising rule only applies to traditional print ads.** OAR 812-003-0120 applies broadly to ads, contracts, bids, and other promotional media. Contractors frequently assume online listings or digital media are exempt — they are not. The license number is required unless the material falls under an explicit exception (like small promotional gifts).

**Delivering the consumer notice without retaining proof.** The most common scenario tested is a contractor who delivered the notice verbally, or who has a copy of the notice but no documentation that the client received it. OAR 812-012-0130 requires proof-of-delivery retention, not just delivery. Lack of documentation is treated the same as failure to deliver.

**Assuming the written contract's required elements are optional because the contract is otherwise enforceable.** A written contract may be enforceable even if it omits required elements. But a contract missing the CCB license number or other statutory elements still exposes the contractor to license discipline, CCB complaints, and lost lien rights. Enforceability and regulatory compliance are separate questions.

**Treating fee amounts as permanent figures.** OAR rule text can be amended. Course references to fee amounts like the $400 new-license fee are accurate as of the cited effective date (July 1, 2025), but test-takers should recognize these are time-sensitive and not immutable statutory provisions.

## Open-Book Lookup Tips

- **ORS Chapter 701** — Start at the ORS 701.005 definitions. Key sections tested: 701.021 (license requirement), 701.026 (subcontractor licensing), 701.091 (Responsible Managing Individual), 701.305 (written contract requirement). Search for "701" or "Construction Contractors" in your reference.
- **OAR 812-003-0120** — Advertising and license-number display. Search for "advertising" or "license number" in Division 3 of OAR 812.
- **OAR 812-012-0110** — Required contract terms. Search for "contract terms" or "812-012" in Division 12 of OAR 812.
- **OAR 812-012-0130** — Consumer notice delivery. Search for "consumer notice" or "proof of delivery" in Division 12.
- **OAR 812-003-0142** — License fees. Look for "fees" or "application fee" in Division 3 to find the current schedule.
- **OAR 812-003-0200 and 812-003-0221** — Insurance requirements and amounts. Search "insurance" in Division 3.
- When a question asks about a "must" — trace the requirement to the specific ORS or OAR section; don't rely on paraphrase.
