A **license status change notice** is the filing a CCB-licensed contractor must submit to the CCB within **10 days** of any change between exempt and nonexempt license status. Source: oar-812-003-0321.

## When the Filing Is Required

The 10-day obligation is triggered whenever the contractor's staffing situation crosses the exempt/nonexempt boundary:

- An **exempt** contractor who begins hiring employees or obtaining workers through a [[worker-leasing]] company must reclassify to nonexempt and file notice within 10 days.
- A **nonexempt** contractor who eliminates all employees and leased workers may reclassify to exempt and must likewise notify the CCB within 10 days.

Source: oar-812-003-0321, ors-chapter-701.

## Consequences of Non-Filing

Failure to file the status-change notice within 10 days can constitute grounds for discipline under ORS 701.098, including civil penalties. An exempt contractor who uses employees or worker-leasing workers without reclassifying faces additional discipline exposure beyond the notice violation. Source: ors-chapter-701.

## Exam Relevance

The 10-day notification deadline is a specific procedural rule tested in the "Hiring and Managing Employees" exam domain. Candidates should know both the trigger (a change between exempt and nonexempt status) and the exact deadline (10 days). Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.
