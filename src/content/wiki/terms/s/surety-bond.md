A **surety bond** is a three-party financial guarantee in which a licensed bonding company (the surety) promises to pay valid claims against a contractor (the principal) up to a fixed maximum dollar amount called the **penal sum**. The CCB and the public are the protected parties (obligees). Source: ors-701-068.

### Why it is required

Under ORS 701.068, every licensed Oregon contractor must file and maintain a surety bond with the CCB as a condition of licensure. The bond protects consumers and suppliers who suffer losses caused by the contractor's failure to perform or pay. Source: ors-701-068.

### Bond amount by endorsement

The required penal sum varies by endorsement class. Residential amounts are set in ORS 701.081 and commercial amounts in ORS 701.084. Source: ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

See [[endorsement-bond-insurance-amounts]] for the full table.

### What happens if the bond is depleted

If cumulative claims paid against a contractor's bond exceed the penal sum, CCB may suspend the license and may require a replacement bond of up to five times the ordinary amount as a condition of reinstatement. Source: ors-701-068.

See [[bond-depletion]] and [[bond-requirements]].

### Bond vs. insurance

A surety bond is not a substitute for general liability insurance. Both are independently required for CCB licensure. See [[insurance-requirements]].
