## Definition

**Domestic water and DWV** covers potable water distribution plus drain, waste, and vent systems that provide safe supply and sanitary drainage in residential buildings.

## Why it matters

Water system failures are high-impact: leaks, damage, mold risk, and occupant disruption. Sound DWV planning avoids odors, gurgling fixtures, and difficult rework after finishes.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Fixture planning and layout coordination
- Rough plumbing and vent routing
- Inspection sequencing
- Fixture trim-out and final functional checks

## Inputs needed

- Fixture count and location plan
- Water heating approach and recirculation assumptions
- Framing pathways and structural constraints
- Local utility pressure/quality context
- Code/inspection requirements

## Outputs produced

- Water supply routing strategy
- DWV and venting layout
- Pipe sizing assumptions
- Inspection records and test results

## Key decisions

1. Distribution routing that balances performance and constructability.
2. Hot-water delivery strategy for wait-time control.
3. Vent routing with minimal structural conflicts.
4. Access planning for shutoffs and future service.

## Basic workflow

1. Confirm fixture plan and utility connection assumptions.
2. Coordinate supply and DWV routes with structure.
3. Rough-in with pressure/leak checks.
4. Complete inspections and corrections before finishes.
5. Trim fixtures and test operation.

## Risks and edge cases

- Long hot-water runs causing owner complaints.
- Venting conflicts in tight roof/attic geometry.
- Hidden leak points at poorly protected penetrations.
- Remodel tie-ins to aging or unknown piping.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland remodel stock frequently includes legacy plumbing conditions that change rough-in assumptions. GCs should preserve contingency for discovered conditions and inspection-driven adjustments.

## Example scenario

A bathroom addition ties into an older house. Early camera/inspection checks reveal limited slope options. The team adjusts fixture placement before rough-in, avoiding slab or framing rework.
