## Summary

Tax Basics is a defined Oregon CCB exam domain, with PSI allocating 4 scored items to this area within the licensing exam blueprint. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

For pre-license prep, the most exam-safe focus is Oregon employer registration and payroll reporting cadence, [[statewide-transit-tax]] withholding basics, and high-level [[corporate-activity-tax]] threshold awareness. Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax, oregon-dor-statewide-transit-tax, oregon-dor-corporate-activity-tax.

This domain also overlaps with worker-status decisions because payroll tax handling differs when a worker is properly treated as an employee versus an independent contractor under Oregon guidance. Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax, oregon-independent-contractors-notice-workers.

## Lesson

### Exam context and scope

PSI identifies Tax Basics as a smaller but explicit portion of the CCB exam. Even with fewer items than major domains, these questions are often direct compliance checks where one missed detail can cost points. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

### Oregon payroll setup: BIN and combined reporting

Oregon DOR states that employers with paid employees working in Oregon must register for a [[business-identification-number]] before issuing paychecks. Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax.

DOR also uses combined payroll reporting systems and publishes quarterly due-date cadence that contractors should memorize for exam use: Q1 due April 30, Q2 due July 31, Q3 due October 31, and Q4 due January 31 (or the next business day when a due date falls on a weekend or holiday). Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax.

Annual reconciliation timing is another likely test point: DOR indicates Form WR and W-2/1099-NEC submissions are due January 31, while other 1099 categories can have a later deadline. Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax.

### Statewide Transit Tax versus district payroll taxes

[[statewide-transit-tax]] is a statewide employee-wage withholding system under ORS 320.550 and is not the same tax type as employer-side district payroll taxes. Source: oregon-dor-statewide-transit-tax, ors-320-550.

DOR explains withholding scope by worker location and residency rules and ties taxable wages to ORS 316.162 wage definitions; DOR also notes self-employment income is not subject to this withholding method. Source: oregon-dor-statewide-transit-tax, ors-316-162.

### Corporate Activity Tax fundamentals

[[corporate-activity-tax]] is a separate Oregon business tax regime tied to Oregon commercial activity thresholds and return requirements in ORS chapter 317A and DOR guidance. Source: oregon-dor-corporate-activity-tax, ors-chapter-317a.

DOR's exam-relevant framework is the threshold split: no CAT obligation at or below $750,000, registration at $750,000 and above, and filing/payment generally when taxable Oregon commercial activity exceeds $1 million. Source: oregon-dor-corporate-activity-tax.

### Worker classification is a tax trigger

Oregon payroll guidance highlights worker classification as a core compliance boundary. Misclassifying workers can lead to withholding, payroll tax, and reporting errors. Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax, oregon-independent-contractors-notice-workers.

## Audio Script

<!-- Estimated listening time: 5 minutes -->

Tax Basics is a smaller exam domain, but it's very practical. Questions in this section often ask whether you know the compliance sequence, not whether you can do advanced tax planning.

Start with payroll setup. If you have employees working in Oregon, the state expects employer registration and payroll reporting through Oregon's system. Know the quarterly due-date cadence and the annual January reconciliation deadlines.

Next, separate tax types in your head. Statewide Transit Tax is wage withholding. It's not the same thing as employer-side district payroll taxes. For CAT, remember it's a separate business-level framework with threshold rules.

Finally, remember worker classification. If you treat someone as an independent contractor when they should be an employee, tax reporting can be wrong from the first paycheck.

## Key Terms

**Business Identification Number** — Oregon employer account identifier used to administer state payroll tax programs; Oregon employers with employees generally obtain this before issuing paychecks. Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax.

**Statewide Transit Tax** — Oregon employee-wage withholding tax under ORS 320.550, administered by DOR with wage scope tied to ORS 316.162 definitions. Source: oregon-dor-statewide-transit-tax, ors-320-550, ors-316-162.

**Corporate Activity Tax** — Oregon business tax framework under ORS chapter 317A with threshold-based registration and filing/payment obligations tied to Oregon commercial activity. Source: oregon-dor-corporate-activity-tax, ors-chapter-317a.

## Common Mistakes

Treating Tax Basics as unimportant because it has fewer items. Small domains still produce scored questions that can affect pass/fail outcomes. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

Mixing up statewide employee withholding taxes with employer-side payroll taxes. These are distinct tax structures and are tested differently. Source: oregon-dor-statewide-transit-tax.

Assuming CAT applies only after $1 million and ignoring the registration trigger at lower commercial activity levels. Source: oregon-dor-corporate-activity-tax.

Ignoring worker-status classification until after payroll begins. Classification choices determine withholding and reporting treatment from the start. Source: oregon-dor-withholding-and-payroll-tax, oregon-independent-contractors-notice-workers.
