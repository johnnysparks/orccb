## Definition

**Heat pumps** are systems that move heat rather than generate it directly, providing efficient space heating and cooling (and in some products, water heating).

## Why it matters

Heat pumps are central to residential electrification and comfort-focused design. They demand stronger coordination between loads, controls, ducts, and electrical service planning.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Concept-phase equipment strategy
- Service sizing and panel planning
- Ducted/ductless layout coordination
- Commissioning and cold/hot weather control setup

## Inputs needed

- Heating/cooling load profile
- Envelope performance assumptions
- Electrical service capacity
- Indoor/outdoor unit placement options
- Owner comfort and noise preferences

## Outputs produced

- Selected heat-pump system type and capacity
- Distribution and controls strategy
- Electrical interface requirements
- Seasonal operation guidance for owner

## Key decisions

1. Ducted versus ductless architecture.
2. Capacity and staging strategy.
3. Backup or supplemental heating approach, if needed.
4. Control logic for shoulder and extreme conditions.

## Basic workflow

1. Confirm design loads and enclosure assumptions.
2. Compare heat-pump pathways for house layout.
3. Coordinate electrical, framing, and line-set routing.
4. Install and commission all operating modes.
5. Document owner operation and maintenance.

## Risks and edge cases

- Oversizing causes cycling and reduced comfort.
- Poor indoor/outdoor unit placement creates noise issues.
- Line-set routing conflicts discovered late.
- Owner confusion about multi-mode operation.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland's shoulder seasons reward well-tuned heat-pump controls, while smoke-season operation increases importance of integrated IAQ planning.

## Example scenario

A homeowner wants an all-electric upgrade with minimal visible ductwork. The GC compares ductless and compact-ducted options, coordinates service sizing early, and delivers a quieter, right-sized system.

## Checklist

- [ ] Electrical impacts coordinated
- [ ] Control modes commissioned
- [ ] Owner mode/maintenance training completed
