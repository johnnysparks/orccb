## Summary

Jobsite Safety is a scored domain on the Oregon CCB pre-license exam, with PSI listing 4 scored items in this area within the current bulletin exam structure. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

In Oregon, contractor safety obligations are grounded in ORS chapter 654 and then operationalized through Oregon OSHA construction rules in OAR chapter 437, division 3. Source: ors-chapter-654, oar-437-division-3-construction.

For exam prep, the highest-yield pattern is to know who has the duty (employer or competent person <!-- TODO: link competent-person -->), what trigger starts compliance (for example fall exposure, excavation depth, or defective equipment), and what action is required. Source: ors-chapter-654, oar-437-003-subdivision-c-general-safety, oar-437-003-subdivision-m-fall-protection, oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations.

## Lesson

### Core Oregon duty framework

ORS 654.010 requires employers to furnish employment and a place of employment that are safe and healthful and to do what is reasonably necessary to protect employees. ORS 654.022 requires covered parties to comply with safety and health rules and orders. Source: ors-chapter-654.

In construction, Oregon OSHA Division 3 is the main rule set for day-to-day field compliance. Source: oar-437-division-3-construction.

### Fall protection and training

Oregon applies its own fall-protection duty language in OAR 437-003-1501, and Oregon's rules state that federal 1926.501 is repealed for Oregon construction use in this context. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-m-fall-protection.

Employers must train each employee who might be exposed to fall hazards under OAR 437-003-0503. See fall protection <!-- TODO: link fall-protection -->. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-m-fall-protection.

### PPE, inspections, and excavation controls

OAR 437-003-0134 requires PPE hazard assessment, proper PPE use, and PPE training. Defective PPE cannot be used. See personal protective equipment (PPE) <!-- TODO: link personal-protective-equipment -->. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-e-ppe.

Subdivision C (including adopted 1926.20/1926.21 provisions) assigns accident-prevention duties to employers and requires frequent and regular inspections by competent persons. See competent person <!-- TODO: link competent-person -->. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-c-general-safety.

Subdivision P includes excavation-specific duties such as safe egress in trenches at 4 feet or deeper, daily competent-person inspections, and protective-system triggers. See excavation safety <!-- TODO: link excavation-safety -->. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations.

### Ladder basics

Subdivision X requires defective ladders to be removed from service and includes setup requirements such as extension of side rails above a landing unless an equivalent safe grasping support is provided. See ladder safety <!-- TODO: link ladder-safety -->. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-x-stairways-ladders.

## Audio Script

<!-- Estimated listening time: 7 minutes -->

Jobsite safety questions are limited in count, but they are high-value because they test whether you can identify duty triggers quickly.

Start with the legal frame. ORS chapter 654 creates the general safe-workplace duty and requires compliance with applicable safety rules. For construction work, those rules are primarily in Oregon OSHA Division 3.

From there, think in four buckets. First is fall protection. Oregon has specific fall rules, and workers exposed to fall hazards must be trained. Second is PPE. If there's a hazard, the employer must assess it, provide appropriate PPE, and train workers.

Third is competent-person oversight and excavation controls. Excavations need inspections by a competent person, and deeper trenches need safe egress and protective systems when cave-in risk exists. Fourth is ladder safety basics, including removing defective ladders from service.

On the exam, the best approach is to map the scenario to the correct rule family: fall, PPE, excavation, or ladders. Then identify the required action.

## Key Terms

**Competent person** — A person capable of identifying existing and predictable hazards in surroundings or working conditions and authorized to take prompt corrective measures. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-c-general-safety, oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations.

**Fall protection** — Oregon construction duties and training requirements intended to prevent employee falls from elevations and openings. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-m-fall-protection.

**Personal protective equipment (PPE)** — Protective gear selected through hazard assessment and maintained so workers can perform tasks with reduced exposure risk. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-e-ppe.

**Excavation safety** — Oregon trenching and excavation controls, including inspections, egress, and cave-in protective systems. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations.

**Ladder safety** — Rules governing ladder condition, setup, and use in construction. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-x-stairways-ladders.

## Common Mistakes

Confusing federal language with Oregon-adopted language. For fall protection questions, use Oregon's applicable Division 3 provisions. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-m-fall-protection.

Assuming PPE is only about handing out gear. Oregon requires hazard assessment, worker training, and removal of defective PPE from use. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-e-ppe.

Treating excavation checks as occasional. Oregon rules require competent-person inspections before work and as conditions change. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations.
