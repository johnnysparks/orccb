## Definition

**RMI duties** are the practical compliance responsibilities carried by the Responsible Managing Individual for a contractor license, including oversight of licensing obligations and keeping required information current with CCB. Source: ors-701-084, oar-812-003-0142.

## Why it matters

When RMI responsibility is unclear, organizations miss status updates, record changes, and renewal tasks that keep licenses active.

## Where it appears in a real project

It appears during company setup, ownership changes, role transitions, complaint response, and renewal windows.

## Inputs needed

- Named RMI and authority boundaries.
- Internal compliance calendar.
- Access to licensing records and change-notification process.

## Outputs produced

- Clear owner of licensing compliance tasks.
- Timely updates to CCB records.
- Documented handoff steps when personnel changes occur.

## Key decisions

- Primary vs backup compliance roles.
- Escalation path for missed deadlines.
- Handoff protocol if the RMI changes.

## Basic workflow

1. Assign RMI responsibilities in writing.
2. Build recurring compliance tasks and deadlines.
3. Monitor triggers for required updates.
4. Submit updates and archive confirmations.
5. Validate active status after each change.

## Risks and edge cases

- RMI departure without a documented transition.
- Multi-owner firms assuming someone else handled CCB updates.
- Delayed record updates after structural company changes.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

In Ashland's small-contractor market, one person may hold multiple roles; explicit RMI task ownership prevents compliance drift during busy field seasons.

## Example scenario

A two-owner residential GC has one owner acting as RMI. After a management change, the team updates internal authority docs and CCB-facing records before the next permit-heavy quarter.

## Checklist

- [ ] RMI role and backup role documented.
- [ ] Compliance calendar owned and reviewed monthly.
- [ ] Change-notification triggers defined.
- [ ] CCB update confirmations archived.
- [ ] Post-change active-status check completed.
