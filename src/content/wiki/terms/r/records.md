## Definition

**Records** are the organized documents that prove licensing compliance, contract compliance, notice delivery, insurance continuity, and project decisions across the life of residential work. Source: ccb-contractor-tools, ccb-required-notices-chart.

## Why it matters

Good records reduce dispute cost, support complaint response, and keep renewal workflows predictable.

## Where it appears in a real project

It appears at contract formation, change-order approval, notice delivery, payment tracking, and closeout.

## Inputs needed

- Signed contracts and changes.
- Required notices and proof of delivery.
- License/bond/insurance status records.
- Payment and communication logs.

## Outputs produced

- Audit-ready project files.
- Faster response packages for complaints/claims.
- Reliable renewal support documentation.

## Key decisions

- File architecture and naming standards.
- Retention timelines by document type.
- Who owns quality control for file completeness.

## Basic workflow

1. Define required document set per project.
2. Capture records at each milestone.
3. Verify completeness weekly.
4. Archive in consistent structure.
5. Run periodic retrieval drills.

## Risks and edge cases

- Missing proof of required notice delivery.
- Storing critical approvals only in text messages without project-file capture.
- Inconsistent folder structures across crews.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

For Ashland residential projects, clear records help resolve homeowner questions quickly and support timely responses if a CCB complaint process begins.

## Example scenario

A client disputes whether cancellation and notice materials were delivered. The contractor pulls timestamped delivery proof and signed acknowledgments from a standardized job file within minutes.
