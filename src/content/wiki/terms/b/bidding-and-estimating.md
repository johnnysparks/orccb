## Lesson

### What the domain covers on the Oregon exam

The official Oregon PSI outline explicitly includes Bidding and Estimating as its own test area with 6 scored items. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

CCB's education taxonomy also classifies bidding and estimating under construction business-practices training, which reinforces that this domain tests operational decision-making, not only arithmetic. Source: oregon-ccb-education-providers.

### Estimate-to-contract compliance sequence

A reliable sequence for Oregon residential scenarios is:

1. Develop the bid or estimate method (for example [[fixed-price-contract]] or [[time-and-materials]]).
2. Track aggregate contract price as scope and cost evolve.
3. When aggregate price exceeds $2,000, treat [[written-contract-threshold]] duties as active.
4. Ensure timing and notice steps are satisfied for the written contract package.

This sequence is grounded in ORS written-contract triggers and CCB notice timing guidance. Source: ors-701-305, ccb-required-notices-chart.

Under ORS 701.305, if work begins at or below $2,000 but later exceeds that amount, the written contract must be delivered or mailed within five days after the contractor knew or should have known the threshold was exceeded. Source: ors-701-305.

### Pricing method choices: fixed-price vs time-and-materials

CCB consumer guidance distinguishes fixed-price agreements from time-and-materials pricing. A fixed price gives a defined overall project price at contract formation, while time-and-materials may leave the final total unknown when the contract is signed. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-to-hiring-a-contractor.

Because time-and-materials totals can expand during performance, tracking when the project crosses Oregon's written-contract threshold is especially important in that model. Source: ors-701-305, oregon-ccb-guide-to-hiring-a-contractor.

### Cancellation and notices after contract formation

After a qualifying initial written residential contract is entered, ORS 701.310 provides a next-business-day cancellation right for the owner, subject to statutory exceptions. Source: ors-701-310.

CCB's Required Notices Chart is a practical lookup for timing and authority references connected to residential contract notices. Source: ccb-required-notices-chart.

For open-book study, the CCB Laws and Rules hub is a fast starting point to reach current ORS and OAR text when you need to verify details. Source: oregon-ccb-laws-rules.

## Key Terms

**[[bidding]]** — The business practice of proposing a project price and scope for client acceptance, tested as part of Oregon CCB exam content. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, oregon-ccb-education-providers.

**[[estimating]]** — The process of forecasting project cost inputs and translating them into a pricing strategy used in bid or contract formation. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, oregon-ccb-education-providers.

**[[fixed-price-contract]]** — A pricing structure with a defined overall contract price at the outset. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-to-hiring-a-contractor.

**[[time-and-materials]]** — A pricing structure where final project total may not be known at signing because cost depends on actual labor and material usage. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-to-hiring-a-contractor.

**[[written-contract-threshold]]** — Oregon's residential written-contract trigger at aggregate contract price above $2,000, including five-day delivery timing if the threshold is crossed after work starts. Source: ors-701-305.

**[[right-of-rescission]]** — For initial written residential contracts, a statutory owner cancellation window generally running through midnight of the next business day, with exceptions. Source: ors-701-310.

## Common Mistakes

Treating bidding and estimating as only math and ignoring contract-law triggers. Oregon exam framing and CCB training categories place this in business practices and compliance too. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, oregon-ccb-education-providers.

Assuming no written contract is needed because the initial estimate was below $2,000. ORS 701.305 still imposes a five-day written-contract delivery rule when aggregate price later exceeds the threshold. Source: ors-701-305.

Using fixed-price and time-and-materials labels interchangeably in scenario questions. CCB guidance distinguishes these models by price certainty at contract formation. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-to-hiring-a-contractor.

Missing cancellation timing after signing an initial written residential contract. ORS 701.310 provides next-business-day cancellation, subject to exceptions. Source: ors-701-310.
