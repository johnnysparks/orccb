**Bond depletion** occurs when the total of all claims paid against a contractor's [[surety-bond]] exceeds the bond's penal sum. Under ORS 701.068, when a contractor's bond obligations exceed its penal sum, CCB has authority to suspend the contractor's license. Source: ors-701-068.

### Reinstatement conditions

Before CCB reinstates a suspended license following bond depletion, ORS 701.068 permits CCB to require the contractor to file a replacement bond of **up to five times** the ordinary bond amount for that endorsement class. For example, an RGC contractor who would ordinarily carry a $25,000 bond could be required to file up to $125,000 as a reinstatement condition. Source: ors-701-068.

### Exam context

Bond depletion is a key scenario type on the Financial Management portion of the Oregon CCB exam. Scenario questions typically present a situation where cumulative claims have been paid and ask what CCB may do, or what the contractor must do before getting the license back. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.
