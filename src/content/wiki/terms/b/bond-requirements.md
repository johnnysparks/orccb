Under ORS 701.068, every licensed Oregon contractor must file and maintain a **surety bond** with the CCB. The bond is a three-party agreement between the contractor (principal), a bonding company (surety), and the CCB/public (obligee), guaranteeing that valid claims against the contractor up to the bond's penal sum will be paid. Source: ors-701-068.

### Amount varies by endorsement

The required bond amount is not uniform — it is set by the contractor's endorsement class. Residential endorsement amounts are in ORS 701.081 and commercial endorsement amounts are in ORS 701.084. For example, a Residential General Contractor (RGC) must carry a $25,000 bond, while a Commercial General Contractor Tier 1 (CGC-1) must carry $80,000. Source: ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

See [[endorsement-bond-insurance-amounts]] for a full table.

### Bond depletion

If total claims paid against the bond exceed its penal sum, CCB may suspend the license under ORS 701.068 and may condition reinstatement on filing a new bond of up to five times the ordinary amount. Source: ors-701-068.

See [[bond-depletion]].

### Relationship to insurance

A surety bond is not a substitute for general liability insurance. Both are independently required. See [[insurance-requirements]] and [[ccb-license]].
