## Summary

Business structure questions on the CCB pre-license exam focus on compliance setup, not tax strategy. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor.

Your chosen entity (sole proprietorship, partnership, LLC, or corporation) must still maintain an active [[ccb-license]] and required compliance items under ORS chapter 701. Source: ors-chapter-701, oregon-ccb-license-page.

## Lesson

### Why this topic matters

Entity choice controls how you register, who signs filings, and how you maintain licensing continuity. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor.

### Common structures in contractor practice

- **Sole proprietorship:** simplest setup, owner and business are legally the same person.
- **Partnership:** two or more owners share management and obligations.
- **LLC / Corporation:** separate legal entity with additional formation and maintenance steps.

CCB licensing still applies regardless of entity type if you perform compensated construction work. Source: ors-chapter-701.

### Compliance anchor points

When choosing a structure, align around these exam-relevant checks:

1. The business is registered correctly for the chosen form.
2. The contractor license is active and tied to the proper business record.
3. Required bond and insurance are maintained continuously.
4. The designated compliance lead remains active for the license.

These steps are reflected in CCB licensing guidance and ORS chapter 701 requirements. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor, ors-chapter-701.

## Audio Script

<!-- Estimated listening time: 6 minutes -->

Think of business structure as your compliance container. The exam usually tests whether your setup supports valid licensing and day-to-day legal operation.

You may work as a sole proprietor, partnership, LLC, or corporation, but the key point is consistent: if you perform covered construction work for compensation, you need the proper CCB license and must maintain it.

When solving scenario questions, ask: Is the entity registered correctly, is the license attached to the correct business identity, and are ongoing requirements still active?

If you can answer those three checks quickly, you can usually eliminate most wrong choices.

## Common Mistakes

Choosing an entity and assuming licensing rules no longer apply. Entity type does not remove CCB licensing obligations. Source: ors-chapter-701.

Failing to keep business record updates aligned with license records. Administrative mismatches can create compliance problems. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor.
