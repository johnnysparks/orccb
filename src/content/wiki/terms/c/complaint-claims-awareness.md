## Definition

**Complaint and claims awareness** is the contractor's ability to prevent, detect, and respond to customer complaints and related claim pathways using timely communication and complete documentation. Source: ors-701-131, ors-701-315.

## Why it matters

Complaint handling affects reputation, cash flow, licensing continuity, and management bandwidth.

## Where it appears in a real project

It appears when expectations diverge, timeline slips occur, quality concerns arise, or payment disputes escalate.

## Inputs needed

- Contract baseline and change history.
- Communication log.
- Quality punchlist and correction records.
- Complaint-intake and escalation protocol.

## Outputs produced

- Faster, more structured responses.
- Reduced escalation likelihood.
- Better trend data for process improvement.

## Key decisions

- Early resolution vs formal escalation timing.
- Single point of contact for customer issue handling.
- Documentation standard for corrective actions.

## Basic workflow

1. Receive and log complaint.
2. Acknowledge and classify issue type.
3. Gather contract, scope, and timeline evidence.
4. Propose corrective path and timeline.
5. Document resolution or escalation outcome.

## Risks and edge cases

- Defensive verbal responses without written follow-up.
- Delayed acknowledgment that increases customer frustration.
- Missing records that weaken legitimate contractor positions.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

In Ashland's referral-driven residential market, complaint handling quality can materially affect future lead flow, not just one project outcome.

## Example scenario

A homeowner disputes completion quality on exterior siding details. The contractor reviews documented scope, site photos, and change history, then schedules a corrective walkthrough with written next steps.

## Checklist
