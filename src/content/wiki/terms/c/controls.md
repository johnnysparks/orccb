## Definition

**Controls** are the thermostats, sensors, timers, setpoints, and logic that tell HVAC, ventilation, and water-heating systems when and how to run.

## Why it matters

Good equipment fails in practice when controls are poorly configured. Controls determine comfort consistency, energy use, and whether owners can operate systems correctly.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Design coordination and sequence-of-operations planning
- Rough-in for wiring and sensor placement
- Startup programming and testing
- Owner training and handoff documentation

## Inputs needed

- Equipment capabilities
- Occupancy and schedule assumptions
- IAQ/ventilation operating goals
- User interface preferences
- Utility rate or demand-response considerations

## Outputs produced

- Sequence of operations
- Installed thermostat/sensor layout
- Programmed settings and lockouts
- Owner quick-start instructions

## Key decisions

1. Simplicity versus advanced automation features.
2. Default setpoints and override permissions.
3. Integration between heating/cooling and ventilation logic.
4. What settings are installer-only versus homeowner-adjustable.

## Basic workflow

1. Define target operation behavior by season and occupancy.
2. Select compatible controls with chosen equipment.
3. Coordinate wiring/sensor locations before close-in.
4. Program and functional-test all modes.
5. Deliver owner training with plain-language defaults.

## Risks and edge cases

- Complex interfaces that occupants disable.
- Sensor placement in poor representative locations.
- Conflicting control signals between systems.
- Lost settings after power interruptions.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Shoulder-season weather swings in Ashland make control logic especially noticeable to occupants. Clear default modes and owner guidance reduce “system isn’t working” calls that are really controls misunderstandings.

## Example scenario

After turnover, occupants complain about stuffy evenings and cool mornings. The GC finds ventilation and thermostat schedules conflicting, updates control sequences, and documents a simplified homeowner mode.
