## Definition

The **CCB path** is the end-to-end sequence for becoming and staying a licensed Oregon contractor: choose your business setup, complete pre-license education, pass the exam, file the application package, and maintain license compliance over time. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor, oregon-ccb-license-page.

## Why it matters

Most licensing failures happen at handoffs between steps, not inside one step. Treating licensing as a path helps you avoid delays, denied applications, and inactive-license risk during active projects. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor.

## Where it appears in a real project

It appears before project marketing, before signing contracts, and again at renewal windows when active jobs are still running. Source: oregon-ccb-license-page.

## Inputs needed

- Business identity and ownership details.
- Selected endorsement/classification strategy.
- Pre-license course completion and exam pass status.
- Bond and insurance records.
- Application and fee information. Source: oregon-ccb-guide-becoming-licensed-contractor.

## Outputs produced

- Active CCB license record.
- Public-facing license lookup status.
- Renewal cadence and compliance maintenance checklist. Source: oregon-ccb-license-page.

## Key decisions

- Which endorsement path matches your intended work.
- Whether your timeline can support exam scheduling plus application processing.
- Who owns compliance operations after initial licensing.

## Basic workflow

1. Define work scope and likely endorsements.
2. Complete pre-license education.
3. Pass the required exam.
4. Assemble license package (entity + compliance documents).
5. Submit, track, and resolve any deficiencies.
6. Operate with active-status checks and renewal planning.

## Risks and edge cases

- Passing the exam but delaying application long enough that other documents expire.
- Working while status is inactive or pending correction.
- Changing entity information without aligning license records.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

In Ashland, permitting and inspections still sit on top of statewide CCB licensing; local approvals do not replace state contractor licensing duties. Source: oregon-ccb-license-page.

## Example scenario

A new residential GC candidate in Ashland finishes pre-license training and passes the exam, but does not finalize insurance and bond documents for several weeks. Their marketing plan launches before active license status appears. The corrected path is to pause client commitments until the public CCB record is active and current.

## Checklist

- [ ] Pre-license education completed.
- [ ] Exam passed.
- [ ] Application package submitted with complete supporting documents.
- [ ] Public license record verified active.
- [ ] Renewal reminders and status-check routine set.
