## Definition

**Concrete and foundations** is the project phase where soil conditions, excavation geometry, reinforcement, and concrete placement are coordinated to transfer building loads safely into the ground. In residential work, this usually includes layout, footings, stem walls or foundation walls, slabs, anchor hardware, and curing protection. Source: oregon-residential-specialty-code, oregon-building-codes.

## Why it matters

- Foundation mistakes are expensive to fix because errors are buried and support the entire structure.
- Early foundation decisions lock in framing dimensions, drainage outcomes, and inspection timing.
- This domain blends technical quality with risk management: geotechnical uncertainty, weather exposure, and safety hazards all converge here.
- For contractor competence, this is where code reading, sequencing, subcontractor coordination, and inspection planning show up in one package.

Source: oregon-residential-specialty-code, oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations, oar-437-003-subdivision-c-general-safety.

## Where it appears in a real project

1. **Preconstruction:** plan review, site walk, utility locates, benchmark/elevation strategy.
2. **Site prep:** clearing, rough grading, temporary drainage, and excavation safety setup.
3. **Foundation build:** footing forms, reinforcement, embeds/anchors, placement, finishing, and cure.
4. **Post-pour transition:** waterproofing/dampproofing (as applicable), drainage, backfill, and handoff to framing.

## Inputs needed

- Approved plans and structural details.
- Applicable code path and local amendments/adopted edition confirmation.
- Site data: topography, setbacks, utilities, and soil observations or geotechnical input when required.
- Crew/trade plan: excavation, forming, rebar, pumping, finishing, inspection scheduling.
- Materials: concrete mix design/submittal, reinforcement, anchor hardware, vapor retarder (for slabs where specified), and drainage components.
- Weather window and contingency plan (rain, freeze, heat).

Source: oregon-bcd-orsc-adoption, oregon-residential-specialty-code, oregon-building-codes.

## Outputs produced

- Code-compliant footings/foundation/slab elements built to plan tolerances.
- Documented inspections and correction close-outs.
- Verified anchor/holdown placement for framing connection.
- Stable, drained, and safe handoff condition for next-phase trades.

## Key decisions

- **Foundation system type:** continuous footing/stem wall vs slab-on-grade vs other engineered approach.
- **Elevation strategy:** finish floor height, step footings, and drainage fall relative to adjacent grade.
- **Reinforcement and embed sequencing:** avoid clashes between rebar, sleeves, anchor bolts, and holdowns.
- **Pour strategy:** one monolithic pour vs staged placements based on crew capacity and weather.
- **Inspection hold points:** when to call inspection so schedule pressure does not force rework.

## Basic workflow

1. Confirm plans, code path, and jurisdiction requirements.
2. Verify utility locates, safety controls, and excavation boundaries.
3. Excavate and proof/confirm bearing conditions; escalate if field conditions differ from plan assumptions.
4. Set forms and reinforcement; install sleeves/embeds/anchors.
5. Pre-pour QC walk (dimensions, reinforcement, clearances, anchor layout, access/safety).
6. Place and finish concrete; protect and cure for weather.
7. Complete required inspections and corrections.
8. Waterproof/dampproof and install drainage where applicable.
9. Backfill in controlled lifts and prepare for framing layout.

Source: oregon-residential-specialty-code, oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations.

## Risks and edge cases

- Unexpected soil variability (soft spots, organics, groundwater) can invalidate assumptions.
- Heavy rain can destabilize excavation edges and contaminate pours.
- Freeze/thaw or heat stress can affect set, finish quality, and early strength.
- Misplaced anchor bolts/holdowns can delay or weaken framing connections.
- Premature backfill can damage green concrete or waterproofing layers.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

- Oregon residential work follows adopted state code with local administration; always verify the currently adopted edition and local interpretation before field execution. Source: oregon-bcd-orsc-adoption, oregon-building-codes.
- In Ashland (City of Ashland/Jackson County context), hillside conditions, drainage planning, and wildfire-resilience site considerations often increase coordination needs at foundation stage even when the structural concept is standard.
- Wet-season scheduling in western Oregon and freeze risk in colder periods can materially affect excavation and curing plans.
- Excavation and trench safety duties are not optional; contractor supervision must align with Oregon OSHA excavation and general safety rules. Source: oar-437-003-subdivision-p-excavations, oar-437-003-subdivision-c-general-safety.

## Example scenario

A small single-family project in Ashland has a sloped lot and winter start date.

- You choose stepped footings to maintain bearing at consistent depth while meeting elevation goals.
- During excavation, one corner shows soft material after rain.
- You pause formwork at that location, document conditions, and request direction before pour.
- You resequence the pour to protect schedule: unaffected sections proceed after inspection, affected section waits for approved remediation.
- Result: short delay, but no failed inspection and no hidden defect locked into the structure.

## Checklist

- [ ] Confirm approved plan set and jurisdiction requirements in hand.
- [ ] Verify utility locates and excavation safety controls.
- [ ] Confirm excavation depth, width, and bearing condition match design intent.
- [ ] Verify forms are square, elevation-controlled, and braced.
- [ ] Verify reinforcement size, spacing, laps, and cover.
- [ ] Verify sleeves, penetrations, anchor bolts, and holdowns are correctly placed.
- [ ] Complete pre-pour QC and call required inspection.
- [ ] Track weather and protect pour/finish/curing window.
- [ ] Complete post-pour checks and corrective items.
- [ ] Document handoff criteria before framing starts.
