## Definition

**Indoor Air Quality (IAQ)** is the condition of indoor air as it relates to occupant health, comfort, and contaminant exposure, including particulates, moisture, odors, and ventilation adequacy.

## Why it matters

Poor IAQ drives occupant complaints and health concerns quickly, even when the home looks finished and high-end. IAQ planning protects reputation and reduces post-handover conflict.

## Where it appears in a real project

- Design choices for ventilation and filtration
- Material selection and moisture management decisions
- Construction-phase dust and pollutant control
- Commissioning and occupant education

## Inputs needed

- Envelope tightness and ventilation strategy
- Filtration goals (including smoke resilience)
- Moisture risk areas (bathrooms, kitchens, crawlspaces)
- Occupant sensitivities and use patterns
- Maintenance willingness/capacity

## Outputs produced

- IAQ strategy notes in project scope
- Filtration and ventilation specifications
- Construction-phase air-quality protection plan
- Owner maintenance and operation guide

## Key decisions

1. Baseline filtration level and upgrade path.
2. How to manage moisture at high-risk rooms.
3. Which IAQ controls are automatic versus manual.
4. How much monitoring data to expose to homeowners.

## Basic workflow

1. Define IAQ goals with owner at preconstruction.
2. Coordinate ventilation, filtration, and envelope details.
3. Control dust/moisture during construction.
4. Commission airflow/controls and verify outcomes.
5. Hand off maintenance cadence and smoke-event guidance.

## Risks and edge cases

- Wildfire smoke infiltration during shoulder/summer seasons.
- High indoor humidity from poor bath/kitchen exhaust usage.
- Occupant disabling systems due to noise or confusion.
- Sensitive occupants needing enhanced filtration.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland's smoke-season exposure makes IAQ resilience a practical design topic, not an optional upgrade. Clear operation guidance for smoke events is especially valuable for owner success.

## Example scenario

A family with asthma moves into a new build during smoke season. Because the project includes IAQ mode guidance, filtration maintenance reminders, and clear ventilation controls, they maintain acceptable indoor conditions.
