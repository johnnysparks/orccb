Under ORS 701.073, every licensed Oregon contractor must carry **general liability insurance**, including products and completed-operations coverage, in at least the minimum amounts required for their endorsement class. Evidence of coverage must be provided at licensure and at each renewal. Source: ors-701-073.

### Amount varies by endorsement

Insurance minimums are set by endorsement class: residential amounts appear in ORS 701.081 and commercial amounts in ORS 701.084. For example, a Residential General Contractor (RGC) must carry at least $500,000 in general liability coverage; a Commercial General Contractor Tier 1 (CGC-1) must carry $2,000,000. Source: ors-701-081, ors-701-084.

OAR 812-003-0221 publishes the operational amounts CCB uses for new and renewal applications. Source: oar-812-003-0221.

See [[endorsement-bond-insurance-amounts]] for a full table.

### Evidence of coverage

To satisfy the requirement, contractors submit an [[insurance-certificate]] meeting the content requirements in OAR 812-003-0200. The certificate must name the CCB as certificate holder, state the policy number and dates, confirm required coverage amounts, and include a products/completed-operations statement. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

### Relationship to bond

Insurance is not a substitute for a surety bond. Both are independently required. See [[bond-requirements]] and [[ccb-license]].
