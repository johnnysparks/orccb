An **insurance certificate** is the document a contractor submits to CCB as evidence of required general liability insurance. OAR 812-003-0200 defines the required content. Source: oar-812-003-0200.

### Required certificate fields

Under OAR 812-003-0200, a compliant insurance certificate must include:

1. **Insurer name** — the name of the insurance company providing coverage.
2. **Policy number** — the unique identifier for the contractor's policy.
3. **Policy dates** — both the effective date and expiration date.
4. **Required coverage amounts** — the minimums for the contractor's endorsement class, per OAR 812-003-0221.
5. **Products/completed-operations statement** — explicit confirmation that the policy includes this coverage, as required by ORS 701.073.
6. **CCB as named certificate holder** — the Oregon Construction Contractors Board must be listed as the certificate holder.

Source: oar-812-003-0200.

### Coverage scope required

ORS 701.073 requires that insurance include products and completed-operations coverage in addition to general liability, and that coverage be maintained at licensure and renewal. Source: ors-701-073.

### Endorsement-specific amounts

The required dollar amounts on the certificate vary by endorsement class. OAR 812-003-0221 publishes the operational amounts used in licensing applications, aligned to ORS 701.073, 701.081, and 701.084. Source: oar-812-003-0221.

### Exam context

A common exam scenario presents a partially completed insurance certificate and asks whether it satisfies CCB requirements. Checking each required field — particularly CCB as certificate holder and the products/completed-operations statement — is the key to answering correctly. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.
