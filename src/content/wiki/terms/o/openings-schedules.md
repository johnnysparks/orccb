## Definition

An openings schedule is a structured list of windows, exterior doors, and key interior doors with IDs, rough openings, handing, performance requirements, and finish/hardware notes.

## Why it matters

Openings errors cause expensive cascading impacts: reframing, delayed dry-in, incorrect hardware, and finish rework. A reliable schedule turns plan intent into purchasable, installable scope.

## Where it appears in a real project

- CD plan and elevation coordination.
- Procurement and submittal approval.
- Framing verification before product delivery.
- Install QA and punch list closeout.

## Inputs needed

- Dimensioned plans/elevations/details.
- Manufacturer sizing and installation requirements.
- Energy/performance targets and code notes.
- Hardware and finish selections.

## Outputs produced

- Master openings schedule with revision history.
- Procurement release list by lead time.
- Framing check sheet by opening ID.

## Key decisions

1. Standardization vs. custom openings.
2. Rough opening tolerances and shim strategy.
3. Performance tiers by orientation/exposure.
4. Release timing for long-lead units.

## Basic workflow

1. Tag every opening on plans.
2. Build schedule with dimensions, handing, and performance fields.
3. Cross-check against elevations/details and structural notes.
4. Lock procurement package and communicate revision control.
5. Verify field framing before delivery and again before install.

## Risks and edge cases

- Left/right handing confusion.
- Conflicts between plan dimensions and manufacturer nominal sizes.
- Late design changes after products are ordered.
- Retrofit openings in out-of-square existing walls.

## Oregon/Ashland relevance

Ashland projects can involve retrofit complexity and weather-sensitive install windows. Strong opening schedules help teams sequence dry-in and avoid permit/inspection delays tied to nonconforming egress or safety glazing assumptions.

## Example scenario

A GC trainee catches that three window tags on elevations do not match plan IDs. The schedule is corrected before order placement, preventing a multi-week delay from reordered units.

## Checklist

- [ ] All openings tagged uniquely.
- [ ] Schedule cross-checked with plans/elevations/details.
- [ ] Handing and hardware verified.
- [ ] Performance criteria assigned by location.
- [ ] Revision control shared with all trades.
- [ ] Field rough-opening check complete before delivery.

## Quiz questions

1. What is the main value of an openings schedule?  
   A) Replace drawings entirely.  
   B) Convert design intent into coordinated procurement and installation data.  
   C) Delay decisions until finish stage.  
   D) Avoid all inspections.

2. A common schedule failure is:  
   A) Unique IDs for each opening.  
   B) Handing mismatch between plan and order.  
   C) Field verification before delivery.  
   D) Revision logs.

3. Best time to catch nominal-size conflicts is:  
   A) After installation.  
   B) During schedule cross-check and submittal review.  
   C) During final payment.  
   D) Never; crews can always trim framing.

**Answer key:** 1-B, 2-B, 3-B.
