For exam purposes, the governing law is primarily ORS Chapter 87 (Construction Lien Law), with a licensure gate in ORS 701.131 and a residential notice-form adoption rule in OAR 812-001-0200. Source: ors-chapter-87, ors-701-131, oar-812-001-0200.

## Lesson

### Statutory framework you are tested on

The tested construction lien framework is codified in ORS 87.001 through ORS 87.093. Questions usually test whether required steps happened in the right order and within required time windows. Source: ors-chapter-87, psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

Core sequence to know:
1. Give [[notice-of-right-to-lien]] when required by ORS 87.021 and ORS 87.023.
2. Complete [[lien-perfection]] by filing under ORS 87.035 (commonly tested as a 75-day filing framework).
3. Send post-filing notice under ORS 87.039 within 20 days.
4. Preserve enforceability under ORS 87.055 (generally 120 days after filing unless suit is brought).
5. Give [[lien-foreclosure]] intent notice under ORS 87.057 at least 10 days before suit. Source: ors-chapter-87.

### Notice of Right to Lien vs residential owner information notice

A frequent trap is mixing two different notices. The [[notice-of-right-to-lien]] under ORS 87.021/87.023 is part of construction lien perfection mechanics. The [[residential-lien-notice]] under ORS 87.093 is an owner-information requirement for covered residential contracts delivered by original contractors. Source: ors-chapter-87.

CCB adopts the specific residential form title "Information Notice to Owner About Construction Liens" by rule in OAR 812-001-0200, and publishes operational notice packets in its combined notices document. Source: oar-812-001-0200, ccb-combined-notices.

### Licensing as a lien eligibility gate

ORS 701.131 generally restricts lien perfection and other compensation remedies when required CCB licensure conditions are not met, subject to the statute's exceptions. This means a timeline-perfect lien scenario can still fail if licensing status is noncompliant during required periods. Source: ors-701-131.

### Consequences for residential notice failures

For covered residential contracts, ORS 87.093 links failure to provide the Information Notice to Owner with major consequences, including loss of the original contractor's lien rights for that contract and possible CCB discipline. Civil-penalty authority is in ORS 701.992. Source: ors-chapter-87, ors-701-992.

## Key Terms

**[[construction-lien]]** — A statutory security interest in improved real property that may secure payment for qualifying construction labor, services, or materials when Oregon lien-law conditions are satisfied. Source: ors-chapter-87.

**[[notice-of-right-to-lien]]** — A statutory pre-lien notice framework under ORS 87.021 and ORS 87.023 that can affect lien validity and amount based on timing and exceptions. Source: ors-chapter-87.

**[[lien-perfection]]** — The process of making a construction lien legally enforceable by timely filing and completing required notice steps, including ORS 87.035 and ORS 87.039 obligations. Source: ors-chapter-87.

**[[lien-foreclosure]]** — The court-enforcement process for a perfected lien, generally requiring suit within ORS 87.055 timelines and intent notice under ORS 87.057. Source: ors-chapter-87.

**[[residential-lien-notice]]** — The Information Notice to Owner requirement for covered residential contracts under ORS 87.093, with CCB-adopted form language under OAR 812-001-0200. Source: ors-chapter-87, oar-812-001-0200.

## Common Mistakes

Treating all lien notices as the same notice. ORS 87.021/87.023 and ORS 87.093 serve different legal functions. Source: ors-chapter-87.

Missing the separate 20-day post-filing notice requirement after filing the claim. Filing alone does not complete all required steps. Source: ors-chapter-87.

Assuming lien rights exist even if licensing status was noncompliant. ORS 701.131 can block perfection and remedies despite otherwise good timeline facts. Source: ors-701-131.

Ignoring residential notice consequences for original contractors. ORS 87.093 plus ORS 701.992 create both lien-right and discipline risk. Source: ors-chapter-87, ors-701-992.
