## Summary

Oregon Building Codes questions on the CCB pre-license exam test code governance and compliance navigation more than deep design calculations. The PSI Candidate Information Bulletin includes Oregon Building Codes as a distinct exam domain and identifies the exam as open-book, using the NASCLA Oregon guide as the allowed reference in the test center. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, nascla-contractors-guide-or-2e.

At the legal level, ORS Chapter 455 is Oregon's statewide statutory framework for building code administration, including code scope, permit statutes, and municipal inspection-program structure. Source: ors-chapter-455, ors-archive-2023-chapter-455-pdf.

At the implementation level, the Oregon Building Codes Division publishes current adopted specialty code editions and interim amendments, while local program renewals are managed in reporting groups under OAR chapter 918, division 020 on a four-year cycle. Source: oregon-bcd-adopted-codes-online, oregon-bcd-jurisdiction-renewals-oar-918-020.

## Lesson

### What this domain covers on the exam

The PSI exam blueprint lists Oregon Building Codes as its own content area and assigns it a limited number of scored items compared with broader legal domains. That weighting signals that test questions are more likely to focus on contractor-facing compliance fundamentals than on memorizing prescriptive tables from every specialty code chapter. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.

PSI also states that questions are based on laws and business practices and that the Oregon contractor exam is open book using the NASCLA Oregon guide. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors, nascla-contractors-guide-or-2e.

### Statutory foundation: ORS Chapter 455

ORS Chapter 455 is the statewide building code chapter and is the starting point for understanding Oregon code authority. It includes statutes on purpose and scope, preemption framework, building permits, and municipal inspection programs. Source: ors-chapter-455, ors-archive-2023-chapter-455-pdf.

For exam prep, this means contractors should know where statewide authority ends and where local administration begins. municipal building inspection program pages and local procedures <!-- TODO: link municipal-building-inspection-program --> operate within this statewide framework rather than replacing it. Source: ors-chapter-455.

### Adopted specialty codes and code updates

The official place to confirm currently effective Oregon code editions is BCD's Adopted codes online page. That page publishes active specialty code editions and tracks interim amendments, including effective and mandatory dates for code transitions. Source: oregon-bcd-adopted-codes-online.

Because editions and amendments change over time, any planning or compliance statement tied to a specific code section should be date-checked against the adopted-codes listing before use. Source: oregon-bcd-adopted-codes-online.

### Permit responsibility in contractor practice

CCB contractor guidance states that when permits are necessary, the contractor or company must ensure permits are obtained. The same guidance warns that permit failures can lead to fines from BCD and, in some cases, CCB. Source: oregon-ccb-contractor-tools.

In practical exam scenarios, this reinforces that permit responsibility should be addressed explicitly in contracts and job startup steps, including who applies, who pays, and when inspections are scheduled. Source: oregon-ccb-contractor-tools.

See building permits basics <!-- TODO: link building-permits -->.

### Municipal program renewals and OAR 918-020

BCD's jurisdiction renewals information explains that municipalities with building inspection programs are grouped for renewals under OAR 918-020 and continue on a four-year reporting-period cycle. Source: oregon-bcd-jurisdiction-renewals-oar-918-020.

Exam relevance: local program structure matters for administration timelines, but statewide ORS framework and adopted-code status remain the core authority points for contractors. Source: ors-chapter-455, oregon-bcd-jurisdiction-renewals-oar-918-020.

## Audio Script

<!-- Estimated listening time: 7 minutes -->

In Oregon Building Codes, the exam usually cares less about advanced engineering values and more about whether you can navigate authority correctly.

First, remember where this shows up: PSI lists Oregon Building Codes as its own domain on the CCB exam. PSI also says the test is open book and focused on laws and business practices using the NASCLA Oregon reference.

Second, anchor yourself in ORS Chapter 455. That chapter is the statewide legal framework for building code administration. It is where you start when a question asks who has authority, how permits fit in, or how municipal programs are structured.

Third, check code editions at the official BCD Adopted codes online page. Oregon code editions change, and interim amendments are issued. Effective and mandatory dates matter. On the exam, and in real projects, using outdated edition assumptions creates mistakes.

Fourth, remember permit responsibility. CCB guidance says that if permits are required, the contractor or company must make sure they are obtained. If that does not happen, fines may come from BCD and sometimes CCB.

Finally, understand local administration. BCD states that municipal inspection programs renew in groups under OAR 918-020 on a four-year cycle. That local cycle is important, but it sits inside the larger statewide framework.

Your exam strategy is simple: identify the authority layer, verify the current adopted code status, and confirm permit responsibility.

## Key Terms

**Specialty Code** — A specific Oregon code volume adopted for a construction category, such as structural or residential work, with edition status and amendments published by BCD.

**Building Permits** — Official permits authorizing covered construction work, with responsibility to ensure required permits are obtained emphasized in CCB contractor guidance. See building permits basics <!-- TODO: link building-permits -->.

**Municipal Building Inspection Program** — A local jurisdiction's building inspection program that operates within Oregon's statewide framework and renews under OAR 918-020 reporting cycles.

## Common Mistakes

Treating one code edition as permanent. Oregon adopted specialty codes and interim amendments change, so contractors should verify current effective and mandatory dates before relying on a section citation. Source: oregon-bcd-adopted-codes-online.

Assuming permit responsibility is optional or automatically shifted to the owner. CCB guidance states contractors and companies must make sure required permits are obtained. Source: oregon-ccb-contractor-tools.

Confusing local administration with statewide authority. Municipal programs administer inspection services, but ORS Chapter 455 remains the statewide legal framework. Source: ors-chapter-455, oregon-bcd-jurisdiction-renewals-oar-918-020.
