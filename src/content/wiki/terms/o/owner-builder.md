In Oregon CCB context, an **owner-builder** is generally discussed as a property owner using an exemption pathway instead of acting as a licensed contractor under [[ccb-license]] requirements, including the exemption framework in ORS 701.010. Source: ors-701-010.

At residential permit issuance, ORS 701.325 requires the applicant to sign a statement that either provides a valid CCB license number (if acting as a contractor) or states the legal basis for exemption (if acting as an owner-builder). Source: ors-701-325.

When the applicant is exempt under ORS 701.010(6), the permit agency must provide the CCB owner-construction responsibilities notice and receive signed acknowledgment before issuing the residential building permit. OAR 812-001-0200 adopts that notice form for ORS 701.325(3). Source: ors-701-325, oar-812-001-0200, ccb-owner-construction-responsibilities-notice.

For exam prep, connect the owner-builder concept to both [[permit-license-verification]] (permit-counter gatekeeping) and [[owner-responsibility-notice]] (post-permit owner obligations), since the PSI bulletin tests laws/regulations and building code literacy together. Source: psi-cib-oregon-construction-contractors.
